# Terminology

**Document ID:** AIWA-STD-01
**Version:** 1.0
**Scope:** All key terms used across AIWA policy documents.

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines all key terms used across AIWA policy documents.
1.2. All documents in the AIWA system must use terms as defined here.
1.3. No term may be redefined at the document level without updating this standard.

---

## 2. Terms

### AI Training Input
Any work used or intended for use in training an artificial intelligence model. AI training inputs are not clearly defined under current law. AI training inputs must be treated as controlled unless explicitly authorized.

### AIWA
AI West Africa. A Gambian-based publishing and media company. AIWA is the copyright administrator, the cultural classification authority, and the mandatory local steward for all works in this system.

### Approved
A decision state indicating that a work is cleared for the stated scope of use. See AIWA-DOC-03, Section 3.

### Approved with Limits
A decision state indicating that a work is cleared with defined restrictions on scope, territory, or use type. See AIWA-DOC-03, Section 3.

### Audiovisual Work
A work consisting of a series of related images, with or without accompanying sound, recorded in any medium. Includes film, video, and television productions.

### Broadcast
A radio or television transmission made available to the public. Includes radio recordings and television audiovisual content.

### Classification
The assignment of a primary category to a work under the AIWA classification system. Classification determines all downstream decisions.

### Classification Officer
The AIWA officer responsible for performing and recording classification decisions.

### Compilation
See Dataset / Compilation.

### Controlled
A status indicating that a work requires additional authorization or review before any use is permitted.

### Copyright Administrator
An entity that manages and administers intellectual property rights on behalf of rights holders.

### Creator
The identifiable individual or group who originated a work.

### Cultural Influence
The presence of cultural themes, styles, or traditions within a work. Cultural influence is a descriptor. Cultural influence is not a classification.

### Cultural Work (Modern)
A modern work that contains demonstrable cultural content or context. The creator retains full ownership.

### Dataset / Compilation
A structured collection of data or works selected and arranged by an identifiable party. Qualifies as a compilation under this framework.

### Decision State
The recorded status of a work at any given decision point. The only allowed decision states are: Approved, Approved with Limits, Held, Restricted, Rejected, Escalated.

### Escalated
A decision state indicating that a decision has been referred to the Governance Board. See AIWA-DOC-03, Section 3.

### Exploitation
Any commercial or non-commercial use of a work, including reproduction, distribution, licensing, broadcasting, digital transmission, and AI training use.

### Fixed Medium
Any tangible or digital form in which a work is recorded or stored such that it can be reproduced or communicated.

### Folklore
A creation of collective origin, with no identifiable author, transmitted across generations within a community. Folklore is not owned by any individual or private entity.

### Folklore-Linked Work
A modern work that references, adapts, or builds upon folklore. The work has an identifiable author. Additional restrictions apply.

### Governance Board
The AIWA authority that holds final decision-making power over escalated matters.

### Government Authorization
Permission granted by a Gambian government authority to use or exploit a work. Government authorization is not ownership. Government authorization does not override AIWA classification.

### Held
A decision state indicating that a work is paused pending further information or review. See AIWA-DOC-03, Section 3.

### Intake Officer
The AIWA officer responsible for receiving and logging incoming works.

### Legal Officer
The AIWA officer responsible for confirming legal compliance and ministry alignment.

### Literary Work
A work expressed in written or printed form. Includes books, ebooks, written texts, and manuscripts.

### Local Steward
The entity responsible for local classification, registration, intake, and rights management within Gambia. AIWA is the mandatory local steward.

### Ministry
A Gambian government department with jurisdiction over a defined policy area relevant to one or more work types.

### Modern Work
An original creation with an identifiable author. A modern work is not folklore.

### Musical Work
A composition consisting of music, with or without lyrics.

### Non-Exclusive
A rights arrangement in which the holder does not have the sole right to exploit a work. PRIME is a non-exclusive partner.

### Offshore
Outside the territorial jurisdiction of The Gambia.

### PRIME
The offshore rights and exploitation partner of AIWA. PRIME is non-exclusive and partially owned by the same founders as AIWA. PRIME is not the local steward.

### Reference Work
A structured work intended for reference use. Includes dictionaries, encyclopedias, and similar publications.

### Rejected
A decision state indicating that a work does not meet the requirements for entry into the system. See AIWA-DOC-03, Section 3.

### Restricted
A decision state indicating that a work is blocked from the stated use due to classification or legal grounds. See AIWA-DOC-03, Section 3.

### Sound Recording
The fixation of a musical or spoken performance in a medium from which it can be reproduced.

### Stewardship Officer
The AIWA officer responsible for managing ongoing rights, records, and payout.

### Submitting Party
The creator, rights holder, or authorized representative who submits a work to the AIWA system.

### Unclassified
A work that has not yet received a valid classification decision. An Unclassified work must not be released, licensed, or exploited.

### Work
Any original creation fixed in a tangible or digital medium that has an identifiable author or originating creator.
