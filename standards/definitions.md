# Definitions

**Document ID:** AIWA-STD-02
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Scope:** Central reference definitions for core concepts used across the AIWA system.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-STD-01 (Terminology), AIWA-DOC-01 (Classification), AIWA-DOC-02 (Legal Alignment)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document provides the central definitions for core AIWA concepts, including work, folklore, cultural work, dataset, AI use, and lexical extraction.

1.2. These definitions are authoritative. Where a definition here conflicts with language used elsewhere, this document controls unless a more specific provision in AIWA-DOC-01 applies.

1.3. These definitions must be read alongside the Terminology document (AIWA-STD-01) and the Classification document (AIWA-DOC-01).

---

## 2. Work

2.1. A **work** is any intellectual creation that is:

- fixed in a tangible form;
- identifiable; and
- submitted to AIWA through an authorized intake channel.

2.2. A work may be:

- a literary work;
- a musical work;
- a sound recording;
- an audiovisual work;
- a broadcast;
- an educational material;
- a reference work;
- a dataset or compilation; or
- a language resource.

2.3. A work does not require publication to qualify.

2.4. Submission of a work to AIWA does not constitute a claim of ownership. Ownership is determined through the rights review process.

2.5. AI training data is not a standalone work type. It is a use declaration applied to an existing work type.

---

## 3. Folklore

3.1. **Folklore** is any expression of a community's cultural heritage that:

- originates from a collective or communal source;
- has no identifiable individual author or composer; and
- has been transmitted through generations as part of a community's cultural identity.

3.2. Expressions of folklore include but are not limited to: traditional stories, legends, oral histories, traditional songs, melodies, rhythms, dances, ceremonies, proverbs, riddles, crafts, and visual art forms.

3.3. **Folklore is a classification category, not a work type.** A sound recording may be classified as folklore if the underlying expression meets the conditions in 3.1.

3.4. **Folklore is not free use.** It is controlled, licensable, and permission-based.

3.5. **Folklore is held in trust for the people of The Gambia.** No individual, entity, or institution may claim private ownership of a properly classified expression of folklore.

3.6. The following are explicitly NOT folklore:

- a modern work inspired by or drawing on cultural traditions;
- a modern work that uses a traditional language;
- a modern work that uses traditional rhythm, style, or proverb;
- a modern retelling of a traditional story where the retelling has an identifiable modern author.

3.7. **Modern work ≠ folklore. Cultural influence ≠ folklore. Modern retelling = modern work.** These rules are non-negotiable.

---

## 4. Cultural Work

4.1. A **cultural work** is a modern work that draws on cultural references, styles, languages, or traditions of a Gambian or West African community.

4.2. The presence of cultural elements in a work does not make it folklore. Cultural influence is neutral.

4.3. A cultural work created by an identifiable contributor is classified as a modern work. It may be classified as Class 1 or Class 2 depending on the degree of cultural derivation.

4.4. A **Folklore-Linked Modern Work** is a modern work that contains identifiable protected folklore elements alongside the creator's own modern contribution. Both the modern work status and the folklore linkage apply simultaneously. This is Dual Layer Recognition.

4.5. A Folklore-Linked Modern Work retains its modern work status. The folklore linkage imposes handling conditions. It does not extinguish the creator's rights in the modern elements.

---

## 5. Dataset

5.1. A **dataset** is a structured collection of data, facts, records, or other materials that constitutes an intellectual creation by reason of the selection or arrangement of its contents.

5.2. A dataset is owned only in its structure, selection, and arrangement. The underlying data points, facts, or language items within the dataset are not owned solely by reason of their inclusion.

5.3. A language dataset or language corpus is a dataset. The language it documents is not owned. The structure of the dataset is owned by its creator.

5.4. A dataset submitted to AIWA is subject to all classification, rights review, and lexical extraction requirements.

5.5. A dataset may be classified as folklore if its contents originate from a collective cultural source and meet the conditions in Section 3.

---

## 6. AI Use

6.1. **AI use** is the use of a submitted work, or data derived from a submitted work, as input to any AI, machine learning, or automated training system.

6.2. AI use is not automatically permitted for any work.

6.3. Classification controls AI eligibility:

- Class 1 works may be used in AI where rights are clear.
- Class 2 works may be used in AI with tagging and defined limits.
- Class 3 works may be used in AI only with special approval.
- Class 4 works are blocked from AI use unless lawfully and specifically authorized.
- Class 4A works may be used in AI only if the verified government authorization explicitly permits it.

6.4. Restricted and folklore-linked materials are blocked from AI use unless explicitly authorized.

6.5. **Lexical extraction is not AI use.** Lexical extraction is a mandatory system function that occurs independently of AI eligibility.

6.6. An AI use request must be declared at intake. The request does not create an entitlement. It creates an additional review obligation.

---

## 7. Lexical Extraction

7.1. **Lexical extraction** is the process by which any submitted work is analyzed and broken into its component words, phrases, and implied meanings.

7.2. Lexical extraction is a mandatory system function. It is not optional and does not require separate authorization.

7.3. Lexical extraction produces derived linguistic data used for indexing, understanding, and system-level learning.

7.4. Lexical extraction does not constitute ownership transfer of the original work.

7.5. Lexical extraction does not reproduce or redistribute the original work.

7.6. Lexical extraction applies to all work types, including restricted and folklore-linked works.

7.7. Lexical extraction occurs regardless of classification. It is not tied to AI permission settings.

7.8. The lexical dataset produced by extraction is system-level data. It is not the original work and is not a substitute for the original work.

## 8. Classification Summary

| Classification | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Class 1 — Original Individual Work** | A modern work with an identifiable creator and no folklore or community-linked restrictions. |
| **Class 2 — Culturally Derived Work** | A modern work drawing on cultural elements but created by an identifiable contributor; does not directly reproduce protected traditional expressions. |
| **Class 3 — Community-Linked Work** | A modern work with identifiable community-linked cultural elements or communal significance; restricted unless formally cleared. |
| **Class 4 — Restricted Work** | A work that is folklore or contains protected cultural expressions; blocked from standard use. |
| **Class 4A — Government-Authorized Restricted Work** | A restricted work with a verified government authorization for specific, defined uses; authorization is scope-limited and expires. |
| **Folklore-Linked Modern Work** | A modern work containing both original modern elements and protected folklore elements; subject to dual treatment. |

## 9. Decision State Summary

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Approved** | Cleared for the stated scope of use. |
| **Approved with Limits** | Cleared with defined and documented restrictions on use. |
| **Held** | Paused pending further information or review. A defined response window must be set. |
| **Restricted** | Blocked from the stated use. Reason must be recorded. |
| **Rejected** | Does not meet entry requirements and is not accepted. Reason must be recorded. |
| **Escalated** | Referred to the Governance Board or an external authority. Destination and reason must be recorded. |
