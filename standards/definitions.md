# Definitions

**Document ID:** AIWA-STD-02
**Version:** 1.0
**Scope:** Central reference definitions for core concepts used across the AIWA system.

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document is the central reference for core concept definitions within the AIWA system.
1.2. This document defines: work, folklore, cultural work, dataset, and AI use.
1.3. All documents and decisions in the AIWA system must be consistent with these definitions.

---

## 2. Work

2.1. A work is any original creation fixed in a tangible or digital medium.
2.2. A work must have an identifiable author or originating creator.
2.3. A work that lacks an identifiable author does not qualify as a modern work.
2.4. A work acquires protection at the point of creation and fixation.
2.5. A work does not require registration to receive protection under this framework.
2.6. A work may be literary, musical, audiovisual, broadcast, educational, a reference work, a compilation, or an AI training input.

**Work types:**

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Literary Work | Books, ebooks, written texts, manuscripts |
| Musical Work | Musical compositions with or without lyrics |
| Sound Recording | Recorded fixation of a musical or spoken performance |
| Audiovisual Work | Film, video, television productions |
| Broadcast | Radio transmissions and television broadcasts |
| Educational Material | Instructional content in any fixed medium |
| Reference Work | Dictionaries, encyclopedias, structured references |
| Dataset / Compilation | Structured collections of data or works |
| AI Training Input | Any work used or intended for AI model training |

---

## 3. Folklore

3.1. Folklore is a creation of collective origin.
3.2. Folklore has no identifiable author.
3.3. Folklore has been transmitted across generations within a community.
3.4. Folklore is not owned by any individual or private entity.
3.5. Folklore does not become privately owned through use, adaptation, or commercialization by a third party.
3.6. The test for folklore classification requires both conditions: collective origin AND absence of an identifiable author.
3.7. Government authorization of folklore use is permission only. It does not transfer ownership.
3.8. Gambian law governs the protection and use of folklore within this system.

**Folklore ≠ Cultural Influence.**
A work inspired by or rooted in cultural tradition is not folklore unless it has collective origin and no identifiable author.

---

## 4. Cultural Work

4.1. A cultural work under this framework means a modern work with demonstrable cultural content or context.
4.2. A cultural work has an identifiable author.
4.3. Cultural influence does not make a work folklore.
4.4. A modern retelling of folklore is a cultural work. It is not folklore.
4.5. The creator of a cultural work retains full ownership.
4.6. Cultural classification is a descriptor. It does not create restrictions equivalent to those applying to folklore.

---

## 5. Dataset

5.1. A dataset is a structured collection of data or works selected and arranged by an identifiable party.
5.2. A dataset qualifies as a compilation under this framework.
5.3. The selection and arrangement of a dataset may attract its own protection.
5.4. A dataset that includes protected works must obtain authorization for each included work.
5.5. A dataset intended for AI training use is subject to additional controls under Section 6.

---

## 6. AI Use

6.1. AI use means the use of a work as input for training, testing, fine-tuning, or evaluating an artificial intelligence model.
6.2. AI use is not clearly defined under current Gambian law.
6.3. All AI use must be treated as controlled unless explicitly authorized.
6.4. Classification determines eligibility for AI use.
6.5. Works classified as Restricted are blocked from AI use unless explicit authorization is granted.
6.6. Works classified as Folklore or Folklore-Linked are blocked from AI use unless explicit authorization is granted.
6.7. Government authorization does not authorize AI use unless AI use is explicitly stated in the authorization.
6.8. No AI use is automatic. Every AI use decision must be documented.
6.9. Reclassification to enable AI use must be documented and authorized.

---

## 7. Classification Summary

| Classification | Definition |
|----------------|------------|
| Modern Work | Original creation with an identifiable author |
| Cultural Work (Modern) | Modern work with demonstrable cultural content or context |
| Folklore | Collective origin, no identifiable author, generationally transmitted |
| Folklore-Linked Work | Modern work referencing, adapting, or building upon folklore |
| Dataset / Compilation | Structured collection by an identifiable party |
| AI Training Input | Work designated or used for AI training |
| Unclassified | Work not yet assigned a valid classification |

---

## 8. Decision State Summary

| State | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Approved | Cleared for the stated scope of use |
| Approved with Limits | Cleared with defined restrictions |
| Held | Paused pending further information or review |
| Restricted | Blocked from the stated use |
| Rejected | Does not meet entry requirements |
| Escalated | Referred to the Governance Board |
