# Classification Matrix

**Document ID:** AIWA-STD-03
**Version:** 1.0
**Scope:** Mapping of work type to classification category and allowed actions.

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document maps each work type to its possible classification categories and the actions permitted under each classification.
1.2. This matrix must be applied in conjunction with AIWA-DOC-01 (Definitions) and AIWA-DOC-03 (Process & Procedures).
1.3. No action is permitted unless it appears in this matrix as allowed.

---

## 2. How to Read This Matrix

- **Work Type** — the category of the work as defined in AIWA-DOC-01, Section 3.
- **Classification** — the primary classification assigned under AIWA-DOC-01, Section 6.
- **Allowed Actions** — the actions permitted for works holding that classification. All other actions are blocked.
- **AI Use** — whether the work may be used for AI training. "Controlled" means explicit authorization is required. "Blocked" means no AI use is permitted under this classification without escalation and explicit authorization.

### 3.10. Folklore

| Classification | Allowed Actions | AI Use |
|----------------|-----------------|--------|
| Folklore | Register as cultural heritage, permit access with government authorization (scope-limited) | Blocked |

**Folklore-specific rules:**
- Folklore may not be privately owned.
- Government authorization is required for any commercial use. Authorization does not transfer ownership.
- AI use is blocked unless escalated to the Governance Board and explicit authorization is obtained.
- Any third-party use must comply with Gambian law governing folklore and cultural heritage.

---

## 4. General Rules Applying to All Classifications

4.1. A work classified as Unclassified must not be released, licensed, or exploited under any circumstances.
4.2. A work may only move from Restricted to Approved with documented authorization and a recorded reclassification decision.
4.3. AI use marked as Controlled requires explicit written authorization before use proceeds.
4.4. AI use marked as Blocked requires escalation to the Governance Board and explicit authorization before any use may proceed.
4.5. No action is permitted that is not listed as an allowed action for that classification.
4.6. PRIME may only act within the allowed actions confirmed in the AIWA referral record.
4.7. Government authorization does not expand the allowed actions beyond what is explicitly stated in the authorization and confirmed by AIWA.
