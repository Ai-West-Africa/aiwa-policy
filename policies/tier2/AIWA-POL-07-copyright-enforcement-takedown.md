# Copyright Enforcement & Takedown Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-07
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 2 — Operational & Platform Policies
**Scope:** AIWA's procedures and standards for copyright enforcement, takedown notices, counter-notices, and infringement response.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-POL-01 (Rights Administration), AIWA-POL-06 (Content Standards), AIWA-POL-11 (Platform Enforcement), AIWA-DOC-02 (Legal Alignment)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines AIWA's copyright enforcement framework.

1.2. This document establishes the standards for issuing takedown notices, processing counter-notices, managing repeat infringers, and coordinating with platforms.

1.3. Effective copyright enforcement is a condition of AIWA's obligations to rights holders and a requirement for maintaining licensing relationships with U.S. and international platforms.

1.4. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Enforcement Authority

2.1. AIWA holds enforcement authority for works in the AIWA system by delegation from rights holders.

2.2. Enforcement authority includes:

- submitting copyright infringement notices to digital platforms;
- submitting DMCA-style notices for unauthorized use of administered works;
- coordinating with content fingerprinting and identification systems;
- pursuing anti-piracy actions through authorized channels; and
- initiating recovery actions for unauthorized commercial exploitation.

2.3. Enforcement actions are taken on behalf of rights holders. Enforcement actions do not transfer rights to AIWA.

---

## 3. Notice Procedures

3.1. AIWA must issue a copyright infringement notice when:

- an administered work is found to be used without authorization on a digital platform;
- a work is found in a channel, account, or service that has not licensed the work from AIWA;
- an administered work has been incorporated into another work without permission;
- a fraudulent claim of ownership has been made against an administered work; or
- a rights holder requests enforcement action and AIWA determines the request is valid.

3.2. Every notice issued must include:

- identification of the rights holder or authorized representative;
- identification of the infringing work and infringing URL or location;
- identification of the AIWA-administered work that has been infringed;
- a statement that the use is unauthorized;
- the basis for the rights holder's claim; and
- a declaration of good faith belief in the infringement.

3.3. AIWA must not issue a notice without verifying the validity of the underlying infringement claim.

3.4. Notices issued in bad faith or without adequate verification are prohibited and constitute grounds for disciplinary action against the responsible AIWA officer.

3.5. All notices must be documented and retained for a minimum of seven years.

---

## 4. Counter-Notice Procedures

4.1. A counter-notice is a formal response by an accused infringer asserting that the takedown notice was issued in error or that the use is authorized.

4.2. AIWA must acknowledge receipt of a counter-notice within five business days.

4.3. Upon receiving a counter-notice, AIWA must:

- review the counter-notice and assess its validity;
- consult the rights holder;
- determine whether the claimed use is authorized, covered by a license, or exempt; and
- issue a written response accepting, rejecting, or escalating the counter-notice.

4.4. AIWA must not dismiss a counter-notice without a written review.

4.5. Where the counter-notice identifies a legitimate authorization or license, AIWA must reinstate the content and notify the platform.

4.6. Counter-notice decisions may be escalated to the Governance Board by any party.

---

## 5. Repeat Infringer Policy

5.1. AIWA maintains a repeat infringer register.

5.2. A repeat infringer is any platform user, entity, or person who:

- has had two or more valid infringement notices upheld against them within any twelve-month period; or
- has been found to operate channels or services dedicated to infringing AIWA-administered works.

5.3. AIWA must:

- flag repeat infringers in the AIWA system;
- notify platform partners of repeat infringer status; and
- escalate repeat infringer cases to the Governance Board for determination on further action.

5.4. AIWA may seek termination of a repeat infringer's platform access in coordination with the relevant platform.

---

## 6. Fraudulent Claims

6.1. A fraudulent claim occurs when a party submits a copyright infringement notice for a work they do not own or are not authorized to represent.

6.2. AIWA must not process a notice where the submitting party cannot verify their rights in the claimed work.

6.3. Where AIWA identifies a fraudulent claim in its own system:

- the claim must be immediately invalidated;
- the responsible officer must be subject to review; and
- the matter must be escalated to the Governance Board.

6.4. Where AIWA receives a fraudulent claim targeting an AIWA-administered work:

- AIWA must contest the claim on behalf of the rights holder;
- documentation of the fraud must be submitted to the relevant platform; and
- the matter must be reported to appropriate authorities where the fraud is material.

---

## 7. AI-Generated Infringement

7.1. AI-generated infringement occurs when an AI system produces outputs that substantially reproduce AIWA-administered works without authorization.

7.2. AIWA must monitor for AI-generated outputs that infringe administered works.

7.3. Upon identifying AI-generated infringement, AIWA must:

- document the infringing output and its source;
- issue a notice to the AI operator responsible;
- demand deletion of the infringing output;
- demand removal of the relevant works from the AI system's training dataset if applicable; and
- escalate to the Governance Board for determination on further action.

7.4. AI-generated infringement does not reduce the rights holder's entitlement to enforcement.

---

## 8. Emergency Takedowns

8.1. An emergency takedown is required when:

- a work is being exploited in a manner causing active harm to the rights holder or community;
- a deepfake or synthetic media incorporating an administered work is circulating; or
- an unauthorized release of a sensitive cultural or heritage work is occurring.

8.2. Emergency takedowns must be initiated within twenty-four hours of identification.

8.3. AIWA must seek to establish and maintain emergency contact channels with platform partners as its enforcement infrastructure develops, to enable rapid takedown response.

8.4. Emergency takedown decisions are authorized by the Rights Administration unit or the Governance Board.

8.5. All emergency takedown actions must be documented immediately.

---

## 9. Platform Cooperation

9.1. AIWA must establish and seek to maintain cooperative relationships with platform partners to support copyright enforcement as its operational infrastructure develops.

9.2. Platform cooperation agreements must include:

- designated contact channels for enforcement actions;
- response time commitments for AIWA notices;
- fingerprinting and content identification integration where available;
- terms for repeat infringer management; and
- cooperation on emergency takedown requests.

9.3. AIWA must review and update platform cooperation arrangements no less than annually.

9.4. AIWA must document the basis and outcome of all platform communications related to enforcement.

---

## 10. Reinstatement Procedures

10.1. Content that has been taken down may be reinstated if:

- a valid counter-notice is upheld;
- a license is confirmed;
- the takedown was issued in error; or
- the rights holder authorizes reinstatement.

10.2. Reinstatement must be communicated to the relevant platform in writing.

10.3. AIWA must document all reinstatement decisions.

10.4. Reinstatement reverses the takedown action but does not extinguish AIWA's right to issue future notices if further unauthorized use occurs.
