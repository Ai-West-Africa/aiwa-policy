# Content Standards & Acceptable Use Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-06
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 2 — Operational & Platform Policies
**Scope:** Standards for content quality, metadata integrity, acceptable use, and prohibited content within the AIWA system.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-DOC-01 (Classification), AIWA-GOV-02, AIWA-POL-01 (Rights Administration), AIWA-POL-03 (Cultural Heritage), AIWA-POL-07 (Copyright Enforcement), AIWA-POL-11 (Platform Enforcement)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines the content standards and acceptable use rules that apply to all works submitted to and administered by AIWA.

1.2. This document establishes what content AIWA will and will not administer.

1.3. These standards apply to content creators, submitting parties, sublicensees, and platform partners.

1.4. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Metadata Integrity Standards

2.1. All metadata submitted to AIWA must be accurate, complete, and verifiable.

2.2. The following metadata violations are prohibited:

- submitting false creator or rights holder information;
- submitting false title, creation date, or work origin information;
- submitting metadata designed to pass works through classification under an incorrect category;
- copying or appropriating metadata from another work; and
- artificially inflating streaming or performance metrics through false metadata.

2.3. AIWA must reject or restrict any work where metadata fraud is identified.

2.4. Metadata corrections must be submitted in writing and documented in the work's record.

---

## 3. AI-Generated Content Standards

3.1. AI-generated content is eligible for submission to AIWA only if:

- the submitting party holds rights in the AI-generated content as recognized by applicable law;
- the AI-generated content does not infringe the rights of any third party;
- the AI system used did not incorporate AIWA-administered works without authorization; and
- the AI-generated content complies with all applicable content restrictions in this document.

3.2. Substantially AI-generated works must be disclosed as such at submission.

3.3. AI-generated content must not:

- replicate the creative identity of a human creator in a manner that substitutes for or displaces that creator;
- infringe copyright in the training materials;
- falsely represent AI-generated output as human-created; or
- misrepresent AI-generated content as a traditional or cultural work.

3.4. AI-generated works are not eligible for cultural heritage classification under AIWA-DOC-01.

---

## 4. Fraudulent Streaming and Manipulation

4.1. The following activities are prohibited within the AIWA system:

- artificial inflation of streaming, download, or play counts;
- use of bots, click farms, or automated tools to generate false performance metrics;
- coordinated manipulation of platform performance data;
- falsification of geographic or demographic performance data;
- creation or use of fake listener accounts; and
- any activity designed to generate royalty payments that do not reflect genuine audience engagement.

4.2. Royalties generated from fraudulent streaming activity must be reversed and recovered.

4.3. Rights holders who engage in or authorize streaming fraud forfeit royalties generated from fraudulent activity.

4.4. AIWA must report suspected streaming fraud to relevant platforms and authorities.

---

## 5. False Attribution

5.1. False attribution includes:

- claiming authorship of a work one did not create;
- misrepresenting a work as traditional or community-origin when it is not;
- removing or altering attribution information on a work;
- falsely attributing a work to a deceased or living creator without authorization; and
- misrepresenting collaborative works as solely authored.

5.2. False attribution is grounds for Rejection of the submission and termination of the creator agreement.

5.3. AIWA must not knowingly administer a work with false attribution.

---

## 6. Deepfakes and Synthetic Media

6.1. AIWA must not administer:

- audiovisual deepfake content that depicts identifiable individuals without their consent;
- synthetic voice content replicating an identifiable individual without their consent;
- AI-generated content designed to deceive audiences about its origin;
- political deepfake content regardless of consent; or
- synthetic recreations of deceased individuals for commercial purposes without estate authorization.

6.2. Deepfake content targeting AIWA-affiliated creators or rights holders must be reported to relevant authorities and platforms.

6.3. Sublicensees must not use AIWA-administered materials to create deepfake content.

---

## 7. Harmful and Illegal Content

7.1. AIWA must not administer content that:

- contains child sexual abuse material (CSAM) or sexualized content involving minors;
- promotes, incites, or glorifies violence against identifiable individuals or groups;
- constitutes hate speech targeting individuals or communities based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, or other protected characteristics;
- constitutes unlawful defamation or false statements of fact designed to damage reputation under applicable law;
- violates applicable national security laws; or
- is otherwise illegal under Gambian law or the law of the applicable jurisdiction.

7.2. AIWA must Reject submissions containing content in the categories listed in Section 7.1.

7.3. AIWA must remove content in these categories from active administration immediately upon identification.

7.4. AIWA must report illegal content to relevant authorities as required by applicable law.

---

## 8. Educational Accuracy Standards

8.1. Works submitted for educational licensing must:

- accurately represent the subject matter they address;
- not contain factually false information on health, legal, or scientific matters in an educational context;
- not misrepresent cultural practices, historical events, or community identity; and
- include appropriate attribution for sources used.

8.2. AI-generated educational content must meet the standards in AIWA-POL-08.

8.3. AIWA may restrict the educational licensing of content that fails to meet educational accuracy standards.

---

## 9. Restricted Political Manipulation

9.1. Works may not be submitted to or administered by AIWA if they are primarily designed to:

- manipulate electoral processes through deception;
- produce propaganda for a political entity without proper disclosure;
- disseminate disinformation about public health, elections, or public institutions; or
- suppress political participation or voting.

9.2. This restriction applies regardless of the political context or jurisdiction involved.

9.3. Incidental political content in creative works is not covered by this restriction. This restriction applies to content whose primary purpose is political manipulation or disinformation.

---

## 10. Enforcement

10.1. Violations of this policy are subject to enforcement under AIWA-POL-07 and AIWA-POL-11.

10.2. Violations may result in:

- work restriction or removal from the AIWA system;
- termination of the creator agreement;
- reversal and recovery of royalties generated from prohibited activity; and
- referral to applicable authorities.

10.3. Enforcement decisions follow the decision states established in AIWA-GOV-02.
