# Educational & Informational Content Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-08
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 2 — Operational & Platform Policies
**Scope:** Standards for educational and informational works administered by AIWA, including accuracy obligations, disclaimers, child-focused content rules, and AI-generated educational material standards.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-DOC-01 (Classification), AIWA-POL-04 (AI Governance), AIWA-POL-06 (Content Standards), AIWA-DOC-02 (Legal Alignment)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines the standards applicable to educational and informational content administered by AIWA.

1.2. This document establishes accuracy obligations, disclaimer requirements, child-focused content rules, and AI-generated educational material standards.

1.3. AIWA operates as a rights administrator for educational works and does not position itself as an academic publisher or educational authority.

1.4. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Accuracy Disclaimers

2.1. All educational and informational works administered by AIWA must include appropriate accuracy disclaimers where the content addresses professional, technical, legal, or medical subject matter.

2.2. Accuracy disclaimers must state:

- the content represents the view of the author and not of AIWA;
- the content does not constitute professional advice; and
- readers or viewers should seek qualified professional guidance for specific matters.

2.3. AIWA does not verify the factual accuracy of submitted educational works. Accuracy responsibility rests with the rights holder.

2.4. AIWA must not represent AIWA-administered educational content as AIWA-endorsed or AIWA-authored.

---

## 3. Professional Advice Restrictions

3.1. Works administered by AIWA must not be presented as:

- legal advice;
- medical or clinical advice;
- financial or investment advice;
- pharmaceutical guidance; or
- clinical mental health treatment.

3.2. Works that address these subject areas may be administered by AIWA only where they carry appropriate disclaimers under Section 2.

3.3. AIWA must not sublicense educational works for distribution on platforms that present them as professional or clinical guidance.

---

## 4. Academic Attribution

4.1. Educational works that incorporate third-party sources must carry attribution for those sources.

4.2. Attribution requirements include:

- identification of the author or creator;
- identification of the publication or source;
- publication date; and
- applicable license or permission basis.

4.3. Works that incorporate AIWA-administered materials in an educational context must credit the original rights holder.

4.4. Unattributed use of third-party content in educational works is grounds for Restriction pending attribution correction.

---

## 5. AI-Generated Educational Material Standards

5.1. AI-generated educational materials are subject to the general AI-generated content standards in AIWA-POL-06 and the AI governance framework in AIWA-POL-04.

5.2. In addition, AI-generated educational materials must:

- accurately represent the subject matter to the level of detail claimed by the work;
- not contain hallucinated facts, invented citations, or fabricated expert attributions;
- carry disclosure that the material was substantially AI-generated;
- not be submitted as human-authored without accurate authorship disclosure; and
- not replicate or reproduce the instructional style or content of an identified human educator without authorization.

5.3. AI-generated educational materials must not be presented as reviewed or endorsed by academic institutions or regulatory bodies unless that endorsement is verified.

5.4. AIWA may restrict AI-generated educational materials for educational licensing if accuracy standards cannot be verified.

---

## 6. Child-Focused Content Rules

6.1. Works intended for audiences under the age of thirteen are subject to child-focused content rules.

6.2. Child-focused educational works must:

- not contain content that is not age-appropriate for the target audience;
- not collect or prompt the collection of personal data from minors;
- not contain advertising or commercial messages embedded in educational content without clear disclosure; and
- not contain imagery, language, or themes inappropriate for minors.

6.3. AIWA must not sublicense child-focused content for uses that expose it to contexts inappropriate for its target audience.

6.4. Child-focused content that includes cultural or heritage material must comply with AIWA-POL-03 in addition to these rules.

6.5. Any work suspected to violate child protection laws must be immediately Restricted and referred to the Governance Board and applicable authorities.

---

## 7. Public Health and Legal Information Restrictions

7.1. Works containing public health information must:

- be based on recognized public health standards or include appropriate disclaimers;
- not contradict verified public health guidance without scientific basis and clear attribution;
- not promote unsafe practices as alternatives to evidence-based medical care; and
- carry disclaimers where content addresses clinical, treatment, or diagnostic matters.

7.2. Works containing legal information must:

- not represent jurisdiction-specific legal advice;
- identify the jurisdiction to which the legal information applies;
- acknowledge that laws change and that content may not reflect current law; and
- advise users to seek qualified legal counsel for specific matters.

7.3. AIWA must Restrict public health or legal information works that fail to meet these standards pending correction.

---

## 8. Educational Standards Compliance

8.1. Educational works submitted for educational licensing in The Gambia must be consistent with:

- the Ministry of Basic and Secondary Education's curriculum and content standards;
- applicable Gambian educational regulatory requirements; and
- AIWA-DOC-02 Section 3.5 regarding national educational frameworks.

8.2. AIWA must consult the relevant Gambian ministry or educational authority before licensing educational works for use in formal educational settings where compliance requirements are unclear.

8.3. Non-compliance with educational standards does not automatically prevent rights administration by AIWA, but does restrict the educational licensing classification available to the work.
