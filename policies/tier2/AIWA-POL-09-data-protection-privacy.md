# Data Protection & Privacy Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-09
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 2 — Operational & Platform Policies
**Scope:** AIWA's obligations regarding the protection of personal data, voice and biometric data, dataset data, and data of rights holders, contributors, and associated individuals.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-POL-04 (AI Governance), AIWA-POL-10 (Compliance), AIWA-DOC-02 (Legal Alignment)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines AIWA's data protection and privacy obligations.

1.2. This document establishes standards for the collection, storage, use, transfer, and deletion of personal data, voice data, biometric data, and dataset data within the AIWA system.

1.3. This document applies to all AIWA staff, partners, sublicensees, and processing entities that handle data within the AIWA system.

1.4. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Data Governance Principles

2.1. AIWA applies the following data governance principles:

- **Lawfulness:** Data is collected and processed only on a lawful basis.
- **Purpose limitation:** Data is collected only for specified, explicit purposes and is not processed beyond those purposes.
- **Data minimization:** Only data necessary for the stated purpose is collected.
- **Accuracy:** Data must be accurate and kept up to date.
- **Storage limitation:** Data is retained only for as long as necessary.
- **Security:** Data is protected against unauthorized access, loss, and breach.
- **Accountability:** AIWA documents its data processing activities and is responsible for compliance.

---

## 3. Creator and Rights Holder Data

3.1. AIWA collects the following categories of personal data from creators and rights holders:

- legal name and identity information;
- contact information;
- banking and payment information for royalty distribution;
- government-issued identification for identity verification;
- tax identification information; and
- contract and agreement records.

3.2. This data is processed for the purpose of administering rights, distributing royalties, and maintaining governance records.

3.3. Creator and rights holder data must not be:

- sold to third parties;
- used for advertising or marketing without consent;
- shared with AI processing partners unless necessary for the administration of the relevant work; or
- transferred outside of the AIWA system without a lawful basis.

3.4. Rights holders may request access to, correction of, or deletion of their personal data in accordance with applicable law.

---

## 4. Voice and Biometric Data

4.1. Voice recordings and biometric data within the AIWA system are subject to heightened protection.

4.2. Voice and biometric data includes:

- audio recordings of a creator's voice;
- voice fingerprints or voice identification profiles;
- facial recognition data associated with audiovisual works; and
- biometric identifiers derived from submitted works.

4.3. Voice and biometric data must not be used for purposes other than:

- rights identification and enforcement;
- fingerprinting for copyright protection; and
- administration of the specific work containing the data.

4.4. Voice and biometric data must not be:

- used to train AI models without express written consent;
- shared with AI processing partners without documented authorization;
- combined with other data to create biometric profiles without consent; or
- retained beyond the life of the work in the AIWA system unless required by law.

4.5. Consent for voice and biometric data use must be explicit, documented, and separately obtained from general submission consent.

---

## 5. AI Dataset Data

5.1. Personal data within AI datasets handled by AIWA is subject to this policy in addition to AIWA-POL-04.

5.2. AIWA must ensure that personal data within AI datasets is:

- limited to data for which lawful processing authority exists;
- anonymized or pseudonymized where possible;
- not incorporated into AI models in a manner that enables individual identification without consent; and
- subject to deletion requirements under AIWA-POL-04 Section 12.

5.3. AI dataset data must not be delivered to AI processing partners with inadequate data protection standards.

---

## 6. Cross-Border Data Transfers

6.1. Personal data transferred outside The Gambia must have a lawful basis for cross-border transfer.

6.2. Lawful bases for cross-border transfer include:

- the transfer is to a jurisdiction with an adequate level of data protection;
- standard data protection clauses are in place with the recipient;
- explicit consent of the data subject has been obtained; or
- the transfer is necessary for the performance of a contract with the data subject.

6.3. AIWA must document the lawful basis for every cross-border personal data transfer.

6.4. Cross-border transfers to restricted jurisdictions under AIWA-POL-10 are prohibited.

---

## 7. Third-Party Licensing Disclosure

7.1. Where a sublicensing arrangement involves the transfer of personal data to a third party, AIWA must disclose this to the relevant rights holders.

7.2. Disclosure must include:

- the identity of the third party;
- the category of data transferred;
- the purpose of the transfer; and
- the protections in place.

7.3. Rights holders may object to the transfer of their personal data to specific third parties in accordance with their rights under applicable law.

7.4. AIWA must not transfer personal data to a sublicensee or AI processing partner that does not meet AIWA's data protection standards.

---

## 8. Retention and Deletion

8.1. Personal data must not be retained beyond the period necessary for the purpose for which it was collected.

8.2. The following minimum retention periods apply unless longer periods are required by law:

| Data Category | Minimum Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Creator agreements | 7 years after termination |
| Royalty accounting records | 7 years |
| Enforcement records | 7 years |
| Identity verification documents | Duration of agreement plus 3 years |
| Dataset records | Duration of the dataset license plus 3 years |

8.3. Data must be securely deleted when the retention period expires.

8.4. AIWA must maintain a deletion log recording each deletion event.

---

## 9. Security Measures

9.1. AIWA must implement technical and organizational security measures to protect personal data.

9.2. Required security measures include:

- access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel;
- encryption of sensitive personal data in storage and in transit;
- regular security assessments;
- staff training on data protection obligations;
- incident response procedures for data breaches; and
- third-party security audits no less than every two years.

9.3. In the event of a personal data breach, AIWA must:

- assess the scope and risk of the breach within twenty-four hours;
- notify affected data subjects and applicable regulatory authorities in accordance with applicable law; and
- document the breach and the response taken.

---

## 10. Rights of Data Subjects

10.1. Rights holders, contributors, and other data subjects have the following rights regarding their personal data:

- the right to access their personal data held by AIWA;
- the right to correct inaccurate personal data;
- the right to request deletion of personal data, subject to legal retention obligations;
- the right to object to processing for specific purposes; and
- the right to data portability where applicable law requires it.

10.2. AIWA must respond to data subject requests within thirty days. Where applicable law imposes a shorter response period, that period governs.

10.3. AIWA must not deny data subject rights without a documented lawful basis.
