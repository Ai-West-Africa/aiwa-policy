# Platform Enforcement & Appeals Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-11
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 2 — Operational & Platform Policies
**Scope:** AIWA's framework for account suspensions, escalation, human review, appeals, reinstatement, and evidence standards in platform enforcement decisions.
**Restrictions:** This document may define policy-level procedural requirements, controls, and standards, but detailed step-by-step operational processes and workflows are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-POL-06 (Content Standards), AIWA-POL-07 (Copyright Enforcement), AIWA-GOV-01 (Roles), AIWA-GOV-02 (Decision Framework)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines AIWA's framework for enforcing platform decisions, managing appeals, and ensuring procedural fairness.

1.2. This document establishes the standards for account suspensions, escalation procedures, human review, appeals, and reinstatement.

1.3. Procedural fairness in enforcement is a governance obligation. Rights holders and submitting parties must have meaningful access to review and appeal processes.

1.4. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Account Suspensions

2.1. AIWA may suspend a rights holder's or submitting party's access to AIWA administration services where:

- the party has violated a material term of their creator agreement;
- fraud has been identified in the party's submissions or royalty claims;
- the party is under active legal proceedings affecting their rights in administered works;
- the party is identified on a sanctions list; or
- the Governance Board has determined that suspension is required.

2.2. Account suspensions must:

- be issued in writing;
- state the reason for suspension;
- identify the specific violation or basis;
- specify the duration of the suspension or the condition for reinstatement; and
- be documented in the AIWA enforcement record.

2.3. Pending suspension of an account, royalties must be held in escrow under AIWA-POL-02.

2.4. Account suspension does not extinguish the rights holder's rights in their works. Suspension restricts access to AIWA administration services only.

---

## 3. Escalation Procedures

3.1. Escalation to the Governance Board is required when:

- an enforcement decision involves a claim above the threshold set by the Governance Board;
- the enforcement action involves cultural or heritage materials;
- the matter involves a legal proceeding or regulatory inquiry;
- the rights holder formally contests an enforcement decision;
- the enforcement decision involves a party with a significant business relationship with AIWA; or
- the officer making the decision identifies a conflict of interest.

3.2. Escalation must be initiated within five business days of identifying the escalation trigger.

3.3. Escalated matters must be accompanied by a complete written record of the enforcement action, evidence reviewed, and decision made to date.

3.4. The escalating officer may not unilaterally reverse an escalated decision. Escalated decisions require Governance Board resolution.

---

## 4. Human Review Standards

4.1. Every enforcement decision that materially affects a rights holder's account, royalty entitlement, or access to administration services must be subject to human review.

4.2. Human review may not be replaced by automated systems alone for decisions that:

- result in account suspension;
- result in permanent content removal;
- affect royalties above a threshold determined by the Governance Board;
- involve cultural or heritage materials; or
- are contested by the affected party.

4.3. Human review must include:

- identification of the reviewing officer;
- review of all available evidence;
- application of the applicable policy;
- a written decision in one of the allowed decision states; and
- documentation of the decision basis.

4.4. The reviewing officer must have no conflict of interest in the matter being reviewed.

---

## 5. Appeals Process

5.1. Any rights holder or submitting party subject to an AIWA enforcement decision may file an appeal.

5.2. Appeals must be filed in writing within thirty days of the enforcement decision.

5.3. An appeal must include:

- the identity of the appellant;
- the enforcement decision being appealed;
- the specific basis for the appeal;
- any supporting evidence the appellant wishes to provide; and
- the remedy the appellant is requesting.

5.4. AIWA must acknowledge receipt of an appeal within five business days.

5.5. The Appeals Officer must review the appeal independently of the original decision-maker.

5.6. The Appeals Officer must issue a written decision within sixty days of acknowledgment of the appeal.

5.7. The Appeals Officer's decision must be in one of the allowed decision states under AIWA-GOV-02.

5.8. Where the appeal involves a claim above the Governance Board threshold, the appeal is reviewed by the Governance Board directly.

---

## 6. Reinstatement Procedures

6.1. Reinstatement of a suspended account or restored content requires:

- a successful appeal; or
- resolution of the condition that triggered the suspension.

6.2. Reinstatement must be communicated to the relevant party in writing.

6.3. Upon reinstatement:

- escrowed royalties must be reviewed and released or further held in accordance with the reinstatement conditions;
- active sublicensees must be notified of reinstatement where relevant;
- the enforcement record must be updated to reflect the reinstatement; and
- AIWA must document the reinstatement basis.

6.4. Reinstatement does not extinguish AIWA's right to take future enforcement action if further violations occur.

---

## 7. Evidence Standards

7.1. Enforcement decisions must be based on documented evidence.

7.2. Acceptable evidence includes:

- platform data, analytics, or reports;
- documented observations by AIWA staff;
- third-party reports submitted with identifying information;
- legal documents and court records;
- financial records; and
- prior enforcement records.

7.3. Evidence must not be:

- fabricated;
- obtained through deceptive means;
- anonymized in a way that prevents its verification; or
- relied upon without the opportunity for the affected party to respond.

7.4. Evidence must be retained for a minimum of seven years.

7.5. Where evidence is insufficient to support an enforcement decision, the matter must be Held pending further investigation.

---

## 8. Enforcement Timelines

8.1. AIWA must adhere to the following minimum timelines in enforcement matters:

| Action | Timeline |
|---|---|
| Acknowledge enforcement complaint | 5 business days |
| Issue account suspension notice | 5 business days from decision |
| Acknowledge appeal | 5 business days from receipt |
| Resolve appeal | 60 days from acknowledgment |
| Communicate reinstatement | 5 business days from decision |
| Emergency enforcement action | 24 hours |

8.2. Material delays beyond these timelines must be documented with the reason for delay.

8.3. Rights holders may escalate to the Governance Board if timelines are materially exceeded without explanation.
