# Compliance & International Licensing Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-10
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 2 — Operational & Platform Policies
**Scope:** AIWA's compliance obligations in connection with international licensing, sanctions, anti-money laundering, export controls, anti-corruption, and restricted jurisdictions.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-DOC-02 (Legal Alignment), AIWA-POL-01 (Rights Administration), AIWA-POL-09 (Data Protection), AIWA-GOV-01 (Roles), AIWA-GOV-02 (Decision Framework)

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines AIWA's compliance obligations in connection with international licensing and cross-border rights administration.

1.2. This document establishes AIWA's standards for sanctions screening, anti-money laundering, export controls, anti-corruption, and restricted jurisdiction management.

1.3. These standards apply to all international licensing arrangements, sublicensing agreements, royalty distributions, and AI dataset licensing activities.

1.4. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Sanctions Compliance

2.1. AIWA must screen all counterparties, rights holders, and licensing partners against applicable sanctions lists before entering any agreement or making any payment.

2.2. Applicable sanctions lists include:

- the U.S. Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list;
- EU financial sanctions lists;
- UN Security Council sanctions lists; and
- any sanctions lists applicable under Gambian law.

2.3. AIWA must not enter into agreements with, or make payments to, any party identified on an applicable sanctions list.

2.4. Where a counterparty is found to be sanctioned after an agreement has been executed:

- all activity under the agreement must be suspended immediately;
- the matter must be escalated to the Governance Board;
- applicable regulatory authorities must be notified as required; and
- legal counsel must be engaged.

2.5. Sanctions screening must be repeated no less than annually for all active counterparties.

---

## 3. Anti-Money Laundering and Know Your Customer

3.1. AIWA must apply Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC) standards to all new rights holder relationships and significant sublicensing arrangements.

3.2. KYC requirements include:

- verification of the identity of the rights holder or counterparty;
- identification of beneficial owners where the counterparty is an entity;
- source of funds verification for significant payments; and
- ongoing monitoring for unusual transaction patterns.

3.3. AIWA must not distribute royalties to an unverified rights holder.

3.4. Where a rights holder fails KYC requirements, royalties must be held in escrow under AIWA-POL-02 pending resolution.

3.5. AIWA must report suspicious transactions to applicable financial intelligence authorities in accordance with Gambian law and applicable international standards.

3.6. AIWA must maintain AML/KYC records for a minimum of seven years.

---

## 4. Export Controls

4.1. AIWA must ensure that AI dataset licensing and technology-related licensing arrangements comply with applicable export control laws.

4.2. AI-processable datasets and associated processing technologies must not be transferred to restricted jurisdictions or restricted end-users under applicable export control regulations.

4.3. AIWA must obtain appropriate export control review before licensing AI-processable datasets to:

- entities in jurisdictions subject to technology export restrictions; and
- entities whose end-use may implicate export control concerns.

4.4. Export control violations must be reported to the Governance Board and applicable authorities immediately upon identification.

---

## 5. Anti-Corruption

5.1. AIWA must not offer, pay, or receive bribes or improper payments in connection with any licensing, enforcement, or business development activity.

5.2. Anti-corruption standards apply to:

- payments to government officials;
- payments to platform representatives;
- payments in connection with obtaining licenses or authorizations; and
- payments to any party in a position of regulatory authority over AIWA activities.

5.3. Facilitation payments (small informal payments to expedite routine government actions) are prohibited.

5.4. AIWA must maintain records of all payments made in connection with licensing and business development.

5.5. AIWA staff and authorized agents must be trained on anti-corruption obligations no less than annually.

---

## 6. Foreign Licensing Rules

6.1. Foreign licensing — licensing AIWA-administered works in jurisdictions outside The Gambia — is subject to this policy and applicable local law in the licensing jurisdiction.

6.2. Foreign licensing arrangements must:

- identify the applicable law governing the arrangement;
- confirm that the proposed use is lawful in the licensing jurisdiction;
- confirm that revenue repatriation is lawful under applicable law; and
- not violate the cultural governance standards in AIWA-POL-03.

6.3. Foreign licensing arrangements involving cultural heritage or TCE materials require Cultural Review Unit clearance under AIWA-POL-03.

6.4. Foreign sublicensing agents must be vetted against sanctions lists and must comply with AIWA's AML/KYC requirements.

---

## 7. Platform Compliance

7.1. AIWA must ensure that its sublicensing arrangements with digital platforms comply with applicable platform regulatory requirements.

7.2. Platform compliance requirements include:

- adherence to DSP content policies;
- compliance with platform metadata standards;
- cooperation with platform fraud detection and enforcement systems; and
- compliance with platform-specific DMCA or equivalent takedown frameworks.

7.3. AIWA must monitor regulatory developments affecting digital platform compliance and update its platform agreements accordingly.

---

## 8. Restricted Jurisdictions

8.1. AIWA maintains a restricted jurisdictions list.

8.2. The restricted jurisdictions list includes jurisdictions where:

- comprehensive sanctions prohibit licensing or payment activity;
- applicable law prohibits the type of activity proposed;
- the risk of AML violation is assessed as unacceptably high; or
- the Governance Board has determined that activity in the jurisdiction is not consistent with AIWA's governance standards.

8.3. AIWA must not enter into licensing arrangements, make payments to, or receive payments from entities in restricted jurisdictions.

8.4. The restricted jurisdictions list must be reviewed and updated by the Governance Board no less than annually.

8.5. The Legal and Compliance Unit must be consulted before any proposed activity involving jurisdictions under consideration for restriction.

---

## 9. Reporting and Monitoring

9.1. AIWA must conduct a compliance review no less than annually covering:

- sanctions compliance;
- AML/KYC program effectiveness;
- export control adherence;
- anti-corruption program performance; and
- restricted jurisdiction list currency.

9.2. Compliance review findings must be reported to the Governance Board.

9.3. Material compliance failures must be reported to the Governance Board immediately and to applicable authorities as required.
