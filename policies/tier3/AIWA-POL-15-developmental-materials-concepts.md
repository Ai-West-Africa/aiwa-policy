# Developmental Materials & Concepts Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-15
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 3 — Specialized Policies
**Scope:** Standards for the handling of developmental materials including drafts, concepts, treatments, outlines, pilot episodes, pitch materials, and other pre-completion creative works.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-DOC-01 (Classification), AIWA-POL-01 (Rights Administration), AIWA-POL-05 (Rights Verification), AIWA-POL-09 (Data Protection)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines AIWA's standards for handling developmental materials.

1.2. Developmental materials represent a category that most rights administration organizations overlook entirely. AIWA administers these materials with the same rigor and care as completed works.

1.3. This document establishes the covered material types, ownership standards, submission requirements, and transition procedures for developmental materials.

1.4. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Covered Material Types

2.1. Developmental materials governed by this policy include:

| Material Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Drafts | Incomplete versions of a work in progress |
| Concepts | Written or documented creative concepts not yet in draft form |
| Treatments | Narrative descriptions of proposed audiovisual or literary works |
| Outlines | Structural plans for educational or literary works |
| Pilot Episodes | Initial episodes of a planned series, in any format |
| Educational Outlines | Structured plans for educational modules or curricula |
| Pitch Materials | Presentations and materials prepared for commercial or funding pitches |
| Rough Cuts | Preliminary edits of audiovisual works |
| Demo Recordings | Preliminary recordings of musical or spoken word works |
| Concept Albums | Collections of demo or developmental recordings |

2.2. Developmental materials are works. Authorship and rights attach at creation.

2.3. Developmental materials are not folklore, public domain, or community property solely because they are incomplete.

---

## 3. Rights in Developmental Materials

3.1. Rights in developmental materials vest in the creator at the moment of creation.

3.2. AIWA administers developmental materials in the same manner as completed works, subject to the limitations in this policy.

3.3. Developmental materials may not be licensed for commercial exploitation without the rights holder's authorization.

3.4. Developmental materials may be submitted to AIWA for:

- protective registration and provenance documentation;
- rights verification and chain-of-title establishment; and
- restricted administration pending completion.

3.5. Full commercial administration rights for developmental materials are activated upon confirmation that the material has reached the applicable completion threshold defined by the rights holder.

---

## 4. Ownership During Development

4.1. Where a developmental material is created by multiple collaborators, all collaborators hold rights in the material from the moment of creation.

4.2. AIWA must identify all contributing parties to a developmental material at intake.

4.3. Collaborator contributions to developmental materials must be documented including:

- each collaborator's identity;
- each collaborator's contribution;
- each collaborator's ownership percentage or basis for ownership; and
- any agreement between collaborators governing their respective rights.

4.4. Where no agreement between collaborators exists, AIWA must ensure the developmental material is Held pending a written agreement between collaborators before proceeding with administration.

4.5. Commissioned developmental materials — materials created by one party for another under a work-for-hire or commissioning agreement — require documentation of the commissioning arrangement before ownership is confirmed.

---

## 5. Submission of Developmental Materials

5.1. Rights holders may submit developmental materials to AIWA for registration and protective administration.

5.2. Submission of a developmental material to AIWA constitutes:

- a request for rights registration in the AIWA system; and
- authorization for AIWA to maintain provenance records.

5.3. Submission of a developmental material does not constitute:

- authorization for commercial licensing;
- authorization for AI processing;
- authorization for public distribution; or
- transfer of ownership to AIWA.

5.4. The submitting party must identify the development stage of the material at submission.

5.5. Developmental materials must be classified under AIWA-DOC-01 at submission.

---

## 6. Confidentiality of Developmental Materials

6.1. Developmental materials submitted to AIWA are treated as confidential by default.

6.2. AIWA must not:

- disclose the existence of a developmental material submission without the rights holder's authorization;
- share developmental material contents with third parties without authorization;
- use developmental materials as examples, references, or demonstrations without authorization; or
- allow access to developmental materials beyond authorized AIWA personnel.

6.3. Confidentiality of developmental materials applies unless the rights holder expressly authorizes disclosure.

6.4. AIWA personnel who access developmental materials must be bound by confidentiality obligations.

---

## 7. Transition to Completed Works

7.1. A developmental material transitions to a completed work when the rights holder confirms that the work meets their completion standard.

7.2. Transition to completed work status requires:

- written confirmation from the rights holder;
- updated metadata reflecting the completed work; and
- confirmation of the applicable rights for the completed work.

7.3. Upon transition, the full rights administration framework under AIWA-POL-01 applies.

7.4. Transition does not alter the ownership of the work.

7.5. Developmental versions of a completed work are retained in the AIWA records as version history. Retention periods for developmental version records are governed by AIWA-POL-09 Section 8 and the applicable minimum periods established therein.

---

## 8. Abandoned Materials

8.1. A developmental material is abandoned when:

- the rights holder withdraws it from the AIWA system;
- the rights holder dies and no estate claim is made within the applicable period; or
- no activity has been recorded for the material for five years and the rights holder has not responded to outreach.

8.2. AIWA must notify the rights holder before treating a developmental material as abandoned.

8.3. Abandoned materials are Held in the AIWA system for a minimum of three years before any action is taken.

8.4. Abandoned developmental materials are not public domain. Abandonment by a rights holder does not extinguish underlying rights.

8.5. AIWA must document the basis for any abandonment determination.

---

## 9. Developmental Materials and AI Processing

9.1. Developmental materials must not be submitted for AI processing without express written consent from all rights holders.

9.2. AI processing of developmental materials is subject to the full AI governance framework in AIWA-POL-04.

9.3. AI processing of developmental materials that contain cultural or community-origin content is subject to AIWA-POL-03.

9.4. Concept materials and pitch materials must not be processed by AI systems in a manner that could expose commercially sensitive creative strategies.

---
