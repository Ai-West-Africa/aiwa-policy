# AI Dataset Licensing Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-12
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 3 — Specialized Policies
**Scope:** Licensing of AIWA-administered works as AI datasets to external AI partners, including eligibility, partner standards, prohibited uses, compensation, and audit rights.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-POL-01 (Rights Administration), AIWA-POL-02 (Royalty Governance), AIWA-POL-03 (Cultural Restrictions), AIWA-POL-04 (AI Governance), AIWA-POL-05 (Rights Verification), AIWA-POL-09 (Data Protection & Privacy), AIWA-POL-10 (Compliance), AIWA-DOC-01 (Classification and Clearance)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document governs the external licensing of AIWA-administered works as AI datasets.

1.2. This document is separate from and in addition to AIWA-POL-04, which governs internal AI governance and dataset stewardship.

1.3. This document defines the standards for AI partner eligibility, approved uses, prohibited uses, compensation, audit rights, and license termination.

1.4. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. AI Dataset Licensing Framework

2.1. AIWA may license AIWA-administered works as AI datasets only where:

- the rights holder has provided express written consent for AI processing under AIWA-POL-04;
- the work has been classified and cleared under AIWA-DOC-01;
- the proposed use does not violate cultural restrictions under AIWA-POL-03;
- the AI partner meets the eligibility standards in Section 3; and
- the license includes the required contractual protections in this policy.

2.2. AI dataset licenses are categorized as:

| License Type | Permitted Use |
|---|---|
| Full Dataset License | Inclusion in AI training datasets, educational indexing, language resource development |
| Limited Dataset License | Educational archiving and linguistic analysis only; no commercial AI training |
| Research License | Non-commercial academic research only |

2.3. The applicable license category is determined by the rights holder's consent and the work's classification.

2.4. Research Licenses exist to preserve legitimate academic and public-interest research access under controlled conditions. They do not convert AIWA into an open extraction or unrestricted corpus distribution platform.

2.5. A Research License may be issued only to a university, public research institution, museum, archive, or another non-commercial research body, or to a researcher operating under documented institutional ethics oversight or equivalent review acceptable to AIWA.

2.6. Research Licenses must prohibit commercial deployment, onward transfer, and any use inconsistent with applicable cultural, community, privacy, or deletion restrictions.

---

## 3. Approved AI Partner Standards

3.1. AIWA may license AI datasets only to partners that meet the following standards:

- the partner is a legally established entity with verifiable identity and ownership;
- the partner has passed AIWA's AML/KYC screening under AIWA-POL-10;
- the partner is not in a restricted jurisdiction under AIWA-POL-10;
- the partner maintains data protection standards consistent with AIWA-POL-09;
- the partner can demonstrate provenance tracking capability for licensed datasets;
- the partner agrees to model auditability standards under AIWA-POL-04 Section 14;
- the partner agrees to anti-reconstruction standards under AIWA-POL-04 Section 15; and
- the partner agrees to deletion standards under AIWA-POL-04 Section 12.

3.2. AIWA must maintain a register of approved AI partners.

3.3. Approval as an AI partner does not guarantee access to any specific dataset. Each dataset license requires separate approval.

---

## 4. Prohibited Uses

4.1. The following uses are prohibited under any AI dataset license issued by AIWA:

- training AI models designed to commercially substitute for the licensed works;
- generating synthetic recreations of cultural heritage materials without separate authorization;
- creating synthetic voice or likeness replicas of rights holders without consent under AIWA-POL-04 Section 10;
- deepfake content creation;
- surveillance, profiling, or tracking of creators or communities;
- use in AI systems designed to circumvent copyright detection;
- sublicensing the dataset to unapproved third parties;
- retaining the dataset beyond the license term;
- combining the dataset with datasets from sources that AIWA has identified as infringing; and
- use in jurisdictions restricted under AIWA-POL-10.

4.2. Prohibited uses may not be authorized by contractual amendment without Governance Board approval.

---

## 5. Compensation Structures

5.1. AI dataset licenses must include compensation for rights holders.

5.2. Compensation structures must be:

- documented in the license agreement;
- proportionate to the scope of use authorized;
- structured to flow to rights holders in accordance with AIWA-POL-02;
- subject to royalty accounting standards under AIWA-POL-02; and
- reviewed against market rates no less than every two years.

5.3. Compensation structures for AI dataset licenses may include:

- upfront licensing fees;
- per-work or per-token use royalties;
- revenue sharing arrangements;
- hybrid structures combining upfront fees and ongoing royalties; and
- research licenses with reduced or waived fees for qualifying non-commercial academic use.

5.4. No AI dataset license may be granted without compensation unless the rights holder has expressly waived compensation in writing.

5.5. Reduced-fee or waived-fee Research Licenses do not waive provenance, attribution, community benefit, data protection, or dataset deletion obligations.

---

## 6. Audit Rights

6.1. AIWA holds the right to audit any approved AI partner's use of licensed datasets.

6.2. Audit rights include:

- the right to request documentation of dataset use and processing;
- the right to inspect model training logs for evidence of compliance;
- the right to verify that prohibited uses have not occurred;
- the right to verify that deletion obligations have been fulfilled;
- the right to conduct or commission a technical audit of the partner's AI systems;

6.3. AIWA must include audit rights in all AI dataset license agreements.

6.4. AI partners must cooperate with audit requests within thirty days.

6.5. Refusal to cooperate with an audit is grounds for immediate license termination.

---

## 7. License Termination

7.1. An AI dataset license must be terminated by AIWA where:

- the rights holder withdraws consent for AI processing;
- the AI partner violates the license terms;
- the AI partner fails an audit;
- the AI partner is found on a sanctions list;
- the AI partner is placed in a restricted jurisdiction;
- the licensed works are reclassified to a category that prohibits AI processing; or
- the Governance Board determines that termination is required.

7.2. Upon termination:

- AIWA must notify the AI partner in writing;
- the AI partner must cease all use of the licensed dataset immediately;
- the AI partner must delete the dataset and provide written confirmation of deletion;
- AIWA must notify the rights holder of the termination; and
- AIWA must update the active license register.

---

## 8. Dataset Recall

8.1. AIWA may issue a dataset recall requiring an AI partner to immediately destroy a licensed dataset where:

- the rights holder revokes consent;
- a legal hold is placed on the relevant works;
- a material rights dispute arises over the works in the dataset;
- the dataset includes works that were incorrectly included due to classification error;
- the cultural review process identifies heritage materials that were included without proper authorization; or
- the Governance Board issues a recall directive.

8.2. Upon a recall:

- the AI partner must destroy the dataset within the period specified in the license agreement;
- the AI partner must provide written confirmation of destruction;
- AIWA must record the recall in the dataset registry; and
- AIWA must notify all applicable rights holders.

8.3. Recall compliance is subject to audit under Section 6.
