# Archive & Preservation Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-13
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 3 — Specialized Policies
**Scope:** Standards for the digitization, restoration, preservation, and archival management of works in the AIWA system, with particular attention to language and cultural heritage materials.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-DOC-01 (Classification), AIWA-POL-03 (Cultural Heritage), AIWA-POL-04 (AI Governance), AIWA-POL-09 (Data Protection), AIWA-POL-14 (Translation)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines AIWA's standards for archival and preservation activities.

1.2. This document establishes standards for digitization, restoration, language preservation, metadata preservation, and educational archive management.

1.3. Preservation is a stewardship function. AIWA administers archival rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership through archival activities.

1.4. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Digitization Standards

2.1. Digitization of physical works for preservation requires authorization from the rights holder.

2.2. Digitization authorization must specify:

- the works authorized for digitization;
- the format and quality standards applicable;
- the permitted uses of the digitized copies;
- the storage and access arrangements; and
- whether AI processing of the digitized works is authorized.

2.3. AIWA must apply the following minimum technical standards to digitization:

- audio works: lossless audio format at preservation quality;
- audiovisual works: high-resolution digital copy with preservation metadata;
- written works: archival-quality scanning with OCR where applicable; and
- photographic works: high-resolution digital capture with color profile documentation.

2.4. Digitized copies must be labeled with the originating work's identifier and provenance documentation.

2.5. AIWA does not acquire rights in the digitized copy beyond those held in the original work.

---

## 3. Restoration Procedures

3.1. Restoration — the enhancement, repair, or reconstruction of deteriorated works — requires express written authorization from the rights holder.

3.2. Restoration authorization must specify:

- the extent of restoration permitted;
- whether the restored version may be separately distributed; and
- the attribution requirements for the restored version.

3.3. AI-assisted restoration must comply with AIWA-POL-04 and must not be applied to cultural heritage or TCE materials without Cultural Review Unit clearance.

3.4. Restoration does not create new rights in the restored work for AIWA or the restoring party.

3.5. Any creative additions made during restoration that rise to the level of authorship must be separately disclosed and attributed.

---

## 4. Language Preservation Standards

4.1. Language preservation activities are a specific archival function of the AIWA system.

4.2. Language preservation materials include:

- recordings in Gambian and West African languages;
- transcribed oral histories, folk narratives, and oral genealogies;
- language documentation and linguistic data;
- mother-tongue educational materials; and
- community language resources.

4.3. Language preservation materials must be handled in accordance with:

- AIWA-POL-03 for any materials with cultural heritage or TCE designation;
- AIWA-POL-04 for any AI processing authorization; and
- AIWA-POL-14 for any translation or multilingual processing.

4.4. Language preservation activities must not be used to commercially exploit linguistic communities without their consent.

4.5. AIWA must maintain clear separation between language materials cleared for AI processing and language materials in preservation-only status.

---

## 5. Metadata Preservation

5.1. Preservation activities must maintain or enhance the metadata of archived works.

5.2. Preservation metadata must include:

- original creation information;
- provenance chain;
- digitization or restoration date and methodology;
- format specifications;
- cultural or heritage classification; and
- any restrictions or conditions on the archival copy.

5.3. Metadata must not be altered in a way that misrepresents the origin, authorship, or status of the work.

5.4. Metadata preservation is a permanent obligation. Metadata records must not be deleted while the work remains in the AIWA system.

---

## 6. Educational Archive Management

6.1. Educational archives — collections of works maintained for academic, research, or educational purposes — are subject to the educational content standards in AIWA-POL-08.

6.2. Educational archive access must be:

- limited to authorized users;
- governed by an access agreement specifying permitted uses;
- consistent with the rights holder's consent; and
- subject to audit to confirm compliance with access terms.

6.3. Works in educational archives may not be copied, reproduced, or distributed beyond the terms of the access agreement without the rights holder's authorization.

6.4. Educational archive access for AI processing requires separate authorization under AIWA-POL-04 and AIWA-POL-12.

---

## 7. Access Controls

7.1. Archived materials must be protected against unauthorized access.

7.2. Access control requirements include:

- role-based access limiting access to authorized personnel and authorized users;
- logging of access events for materials classified as restricted or culturally sensitive;
- encryption of digital archives in storage and in transit;
- regular review of access authorizations; and
- annual security assessment of archive systems.

7.3. Materials classified as sacred, ceremonial, or community-restricted under AIWA-POL-16 must be held in restricted-access archives separate from general archival collections.

7.4. No publicly accessible copy of a restricted archive may be made without Governance Board authorization.

---

## 8. Preservation Ethics

8.1. Preservation activities must respect the cultural context of the works being preserved.

8.2. AIWA must not:

- use preservation as a mechanism to aggregate cultural materials for commercial exploitation beyond the rights holder's consent;
- distribute archival copies of restricted materials without authorization;
- create preservation records that misrepresent the cultural or community origins of materials;
- remove cultural context or attribution from archival copies; or
- use preserved materials in ways not contemplated by the rights holder at the time of preservation authorization.

8.3. Community consultation is required before initiating large-scale preservation projects involving community-origin materials.

8.4. Preservation of sacred or ceremonially significant materials must be approved by the Cultural Review Unit and, where applicable, the relevant community authority.
