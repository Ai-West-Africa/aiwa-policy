# Sensitive Cultural Materials Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-16
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 3 — Specialized Policies
**Scope:** Standards for the handling of sacred works, ceremonial recordings, indigenous archives, oral genealogies, and restricted traditional materials within the AIWA system.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-DOC-01 (Classification), AIWA-DOC-02 (Legal Alignment), AIWA-POL-03 (Cultural Heritage), AIWA-POL-04 (AI Governance), AIWA-POL-13 (Archive & Preservation), AIWA-GOV-02 (Decision Framework)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines the standards applicable to sensitive cultural materials within the AIWA system.

1.2. This document is a specialized subset of AIWA-POL-03 and governs the most restricted categories of cultural material.

1.3. Sensitive cultural materials require handling standards above and beyond those applicable to general cultural and heritage materials.

1.4. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Definition of Sensitive Cultural Materials

2.1. Sensitive cultural materials are materials that:

- carry sacred, ceremonial, or spiritually significant status within a community;
- are held under restricted conditions by community custodians;
- document indigenous genealogies, lineages, or community histories;
- relate to initiation, burial, healing, or other culturally restricted practices;
- have been identified by a community authority as requiring restricted handling;
- are subject to prohibition on reproduction or distribution under community law or custom; or
- are designated as sensitive by applicable Gambian authority.

2.2. The designation of a material as sensitive is made by:

- the submitting community authority;
- the Cultural Review Unit following review;
- a relevant Gambian institution such as the NCAC; or
- the Governance Board.

2.3. Sensitive cultural material designation is not applied solely on the basis of AIWA's assessment. Community input is required where practicable.

---

## 3. Sacred Works

3.1. Sacred works are materials that are used in or are intrinsically connected to religious or spiritual practice.

3.2. Sacred works may include:

- ceremonial songs, chants, or musical forms;
- sacred texts or scriptures used in community practice;
- devotional recordings;
- ritual scripts or instructions; and
- materials considered spiritually active or protected by community tradition.

3.3. Sacred works must not be:

- commercially licensed without written authorization from the relevant religious or spiritual authority;
- used in AI processing of any kind;
- subjected to archival digitization without express community authorization;
- published or distributed without the relevant authority's consent; or
- used in advertising, entertainment, or commercial contexts.

3.4. AIWA must consult the relevant spiritual or religious authority before taking any action in relation to sacred works.

---

## 4. Ceremonial Recordings

4.1. Ceremonial recordings are audio or audiovisual recordings of ceremonies, rituals, or culturally controlled events.

4.2. Ceremonial recordings may only be submitted to AIWA by an authorized representative of the relevant community or institution.

4.3. Ceremonial recordings must not be:

- publicly distributed;
- sublicensed to third parties without community authorization;
- used for AI processing;
- used for educational purposes outside the community without authorization; or
- reproduced in any format without the community's express written consent.

4.4. Ceremonial recordings must be stored in restricted-access archives under AIWA-POL-13.

4.5. Access to ceremonial recordings within AIWA is limited to designated Cultural Review Unit officers with documented authorization.

---

## 5. Indigenous Archives

5.1. Indigenous archives are collections of materials documenting indigenous or community life, history, or culture, held or submitted for preservation purposes.

5.2. Indigenous archives submitted to AIWA must be accompanied by:

- documentation of the submitting authority's relationship to the community;
- community consent documentation; and
- clear instructions on permitted and prohibited uses.

5.3. Indigenous archives must not be:

- combined with commercial archives without authorization;
- processed by AI systems without separate consent from the community;
- distributed publicly without community authorization; or
- used for research purposes without a documented research agreement with the community.

5.4. AIWA must maintain indigenous archives in separate, secured archive holdings.

5.5. Access to indigenous archives is governed by the community's consent documentation and must be managed by the Cultural Review Unit.

---

## 6. Oral Genealogies

6.1. Oral genealogies are recordings or transcriptions of community lineage histories, ancestral records, and family heritage narratives.

6.2. Oral genealogies are held in trust for the families and communities they document.

6.3. Oral genealogies must not be:

- publicly distributed without the authorization of the families or community concerned;
- used for AI processing;
- incorporated into commercial datasets;
- used for biometric identification or genetic inferencing; or
- disclosed to third parties without the authorization of the family or community concerned.

6.4. Where the families or communities to whom an oral genealogy belongs cannot be identified, the oral genealogy must be classified as Restricted.

---

## 7. Restricted Traditional Materials

7.1. Restricted traditional materials are materials held under community restrictions that limit their circulation, reproduction, or use.

7.2. Restrictions may apply based on:

- gender restrictions — materials restricted to specific genders within the community;
- age or initiation restrictions — materials restricted to initiated members;
- seasonal restrictions — materials permitted for use only at specific times;
- geographic restrictions — materials restricted from use outside the originating community; and
- reproduction restrictions — materials where any copying is prohibited by community custom.

7.3. AIWA must honor community-imposed restrictions as conditions of administration.

7.4. Community-imposed restrictions may not be overridden by a commercial licensing agreement.

7.5. Where the exact nature of a community restriction is unclear, AIWA must treat the material as fully restricted pending clarification.

---

## 8. Review Procedures

8.1. All materials designated or proposed as sensitive cultural materials must be reviewed by the Cultural Review Unit before any administrative action.

8.2. The Cultural Review Unit must:

- confirm the sensitivity designation with reference to community input;
- document the basis for the designation;
- assign the applicable restrictions in the AIWA system;
- record the review in the work's governance file; and
- report the designation to the Governance Board for materials above the threshold defined by the Governance Board.

8.3. Review decisions are documented using the decision states in AIWA-GOV-02.

8.4. No action may be taken on a sensitive cultural material before Cultural Review Unit clearance.

8.5. Sensitive cultural material reviews may be escalated to the Governance Board at any stage.

---

## 9. Access Restrictions

9.1. Access to sensitive cultural materials within AIWA is restricted to designated personnel.

9.2. Access must be:

- role-based with minimum necessary access;
- subject to confidentiality obligations;
- logged for all access events;
- revocable immediately upon identification of an access violation; and
- reviewed annually.

9.3. No sensitive cultural material may be shared outside AIWA without written authorization from the relevant community authority and the Governance Board.

9.4. Access restrictions apply to AIWA staff, sublicensees, AI processing partners, and any other party that may handle AIWA materials.

---

## 10. Destruction and Recall

10.1. Sensitive cultural materials must be subject to recall or destruction where:

- the community withdraws consent;
- a Gambian authority requires recall;
- the material has been found to have been submitted without proper community authorization; or
- the Governance Board determines that retention creates unacceptable cultural or legal risk.

10.2. Destruction of sensitive cultural materials must be:

- authorized in writing by the Governance Board;
- documented;
- conducted in a manner respectful of the material's cultural status where practicable; and
- confirmed in a destruction record.

10.3. Recall from sublicensees and partners follows the dataset recall procedures in AIWA-POL-12 Section 8.

10.4. AIWA must notify the relevant community authority of any destruction of sensitive cultural materials.
