# Translation & Localization Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-14
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 3 — Specialized Policies
**Scope:** Standards for the translation, localization, and multilingual processing of works in the AIWA system, including attribution, AI translation governance, and cultural context safeguards.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-DOC-01 (Classification), AIWA-POL-03 (Cultural Heritage), AIWA-POL-04 (AI Governance), AIWA-POL-13 (Archive & Preservation)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines AIWA's standards for the translation and localization of works in the AIWA system.

1.2. This document is particularly important because mother-tongue content in Gambian and West African languages becomes multilingual data through translation and localization activities.

1.3. This document establishes translation rights, attribution requirements, AI translation governance, and cultural context safeguards.

1.4. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Translation Rights

2.1. Translation of a work is a derivative work right.

2.2. Translation of a work requires:

- express written authorization from the rights holder; and
- a documented scope specifying the target language(s) and intended use.

2.3. AIWA must not authorize translation of a work without the rights holder's consent.

2.4. Translation authorization must specify:

- the source work;
- the target language or languages;
- the permitted uses of the translation;
- the attribution requirements for the translation;
- whether AI translation tools may be used;
- the territory or territories in which the translation may be used; and
- the duration of the translation authorization.

2.5. Translations of cultural heritage or TCE materials require Cultural Review Unit clearance in addition to rights holder consent.

---

## 3. Attribution Requirements

3.1. Every translation must carry attribution for:

- the original author or rights holder of the source work; and
- the translator or translation entity.

3.2. Attribution must appear in a form that is visible to the end user of the translation.

3.3. Attribution must not misrepresent the translated work as an original work in the target language.

3.4. Where a translation of a cultural work is produced, attribution must include acknowledgment of the cultural tradition or community of origin where applicable.

3.5. Attribution requirements apply to AI-assisted and AI-generated translations as well as human translations.

---

## 4. AI Translation Governance

4.1. AI translation tools may be used to translate works in the AIWA system only where:

- the rights holder has authorized AI translation in the translation authorization;
- the AI translation tool does not incorporate AIWA-administered works in its training without separate authorization;
- the AI translation output is reviewed for accuracy and cultural appropriateness before use; and
- the use of AI translation is disclosed.

4.2. AI translation must not be used for:

- translation of works classified as sacred, ceremonially significant, or community-restricted without Cultural Review Unit clearance;
- translation of folklore or TCE materials without separate cultural authorization;
- commercial publication without human editorial review; or
- translation into a language where cultural context preservation cannot be verified.

4.3. AI-translated outputs are subject to human quality review before distribution.

4.4. AIWA must not represent AI-translated content as professionally or academically translated without human review and verification.

---

## 5. Cultural Context Safeguards

5.1. Translation of works with cultural, heritage, or community-linked content must preserve cultural context.

5.2. Cultural context preservation requires:

- retention of culturally specific terminology with appropriate explanatory notes where necessary;
- avoidance of cultural adaptations that misrepresent the source community or tradition;
- consultation with a speaker or cultural authority from the source community where practicable; and
- documentation of any cultural adaptations made during translation.

5.3. Translation must not:

- remove or minimize the cultural origin of the work;
- adapt cultural practices in a manner that misrepresents them to the target audience;
- use translation as a mechanism to appropriate cultural material into a different cultural framework; or
- alter the meaning or cultural significance of sacred or ceremonial content.

5.4. Where cultural context cannot be adequately preserved in a translation, the translation must carry a written disclaimer explaining the cultural limitations.

---

## 6. Quality Standards

6.1. Translations produced or authorized under AIWA must meet the following quality standards:

- the translation accurately represents the meaning of the source work;
- the translation is reviewed for linguistic accuracy by a qualified reviewer;
- cultural adaptations are documented;
- the translation is consistent with applicable educational or publication standards if intended for those uses; and
- the translation is reviewed by a native speaker of the target language before commercial publication.

6.2. AI-generated translations require additional human quality review before meeting these standards.

6.3. AIWA may restrict licensing of translations that do not meet quality standards.

---

## 7. Version Control for Translations

7.1. Each translation of a work is a separate version requiring its own record in the AIWA system.

7.2. Translation version records must include:

- the source work identifier;
- the target language;
- the translator or translation entity;
- the translation date;
- the review status;
- the authorized uses and territory;
- any cultural adaptations documented; and
- the rights holder's authorization.

7.3. Revisions to an existing translation are treated as new versions.

7.4. AIWA must not distribute an outdated translation where a corrected or updated version exists without the rights holder's authorization.

---

## 8. Localization Standards

8.1. Localization — adaptation of a work for a specific local market including cultural, linguistic, and format adaptation — is subject to the same authorization requirements as translation.

8.2. Localization activities must not:

- alter the essential creative identity of the work;
- misrepresent the origin, authorship, or cultural context of the work;
- adapt cultural content in a manner inconsistent with the source community's values; or
- remove attribution from the localized version.

8.3. Localization of educational works must comply with AIWA-POL-08 in the target jurisdiction.

8.4. Localized works are separate versions and must be registered as such in the AIWA system.
