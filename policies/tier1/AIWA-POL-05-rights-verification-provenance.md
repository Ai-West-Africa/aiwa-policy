# Rights Verification & Provenance Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-05
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 1 — Foundational Governance
**Scope:** Standards for verifying ownership, provenance, chain of title, and rights status for all works entering or held in the AIWA system.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-DOC-01 (Classification), AIWA-DOC-02 (Legal Alignment), AIWA-DOC-03 (Process & Procedures), AIWA-POL-01 (Rights Administration), AIWA-POL-04 (AI Governance), AIWA-GOV-02 (Decision Framework)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines the standards AIWA applies to verify ownership, provenance, and rights status for works in the AIWA system.

1.2. This document is the chain-of-title infrastructure for the AIWA rights administration system.

1.3. Verified provenance is a prerequisite for any licensing, distribution, AI processing, or exploitation of a work.

1.4. No work may be cleared for use without passing the rights verification and provenance standards in this document.

1.5. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Ownership Verification

2.1. AIWA must verify ownership of every work submitted to the system before granting any administrative, licensing, or processing rights.

2.2. Ownership verification must establish:

- who created the work;
- whether the creator is the rights holder, or whether rights have been transferred;
- the current rights holder's identity;
- the documentation supporting the ownership claim; and
- whether any competing ownership claims exist.

2.3. AIWA must not accept a self-certification of ownership as the sole basis for ownership verification for works submitted for commercial exploitation.

2.4. Where documentary evidence of ownership is unavailable, the work must be Held pending further verification.

2.5. AIWA must maintain a rights holder register for all verified works in the system.

---

## 3. Contributor Identification

3.1. Every person or entity who submits a work to the AIWA system must be identified.

3.2. Contributor identification requires:

- legal name;
- identification number (national ID, passport, or equivalent);
- contact information;
- country of residence; and
- confirmation of their relationship to the work (creator, rights holder, authorized agent).

3.3. AIWA must verify contributor identity before processing the submission.

3.4. Anonymous submissions are not permitted for works submitted for commercial administration.

3.5. Submissions by agents or third parties require:

- the agent's identification;
- documentation of the agent's authority to submit on behalf of the rights holder; and
- the rights holder's identified contact information.

3.6. Contributor authenticity is a foundational submission requirement. A contributor may only submit to AIWA works they created, works they own, or works for which they hold documented authority to submit on behalf of the verified rights holder. Submitting a work without legitimate authority to submit it is a material breach of AIWA's submission requirements and of any creator agreement in force.

3.7. AIWA's governance legitimacy depends on the integrity of contributor submissions. Contributors bear primary responsibility for the authenticity of their submissions. AIWA relies on contributor declarations made at intake, and those declarations transfer primary submission responsibility to the contributor.

---

## 4. Split Ownership Handling

4.1. Split ownership occurs when a work is owned by more than one rights holder.

4.2. AIWA must identify all co-owners of a split-ownership work before administration begins.

4.3. Split ownership records must include:

- each co-owner's identity;
- each co-owner's ownership percentage;
- the basis for the split (agreement, collaboration, inheritance, or other); and
- any limitations on individual co-owners' licensing authority.

4.4. AIWA must not license a split-ownership work in a manner that exceeds the licensing authority of the submitting party.

4.5. Where a co-owner has not consented to a particular use, that use may not be authorized by the other co-owners through AIWA unless the co-ownership agreement expressly grants such authority.

4.6. Split ownership disputes must be escalated to the Governance Board.

---

## 5. Developmental Materials Verification

5.1. Developmental materials — drafts, concepts, treatments, outlines, pilot episodes, pitch materials — require verification before administration.

5.2. Verification for developmental materials must establish:

- the creator's identity;
- the date of creation;
- the current development status of the material;
- whether the material is an original creation or a development of a pre-existing work; and
- whether any third parties have contributed to the material.

5.3. Third-party contributions to developmental materials must be disclosed at submission.

5.4. Developmental materials derived from pre-existing works require verification of the rights to the underlying works.

5.5. Developmental materials rights and procedures are governed by AIWA-POL-15.

---

## 6. Metadata Integrity

6.1. Every work in the AIWA system must have complete and accurate metadata records.

6.2. Required metadata includes:

- title;
- creator name(s);
- rights holder name(s);
- work type and classification;
- creation date;
- language(s) of the work;
- country of origin;
- any known prior publications or distributions;
- applicable rights restrictions; and
- provenance documentation references.

6.3. Metadata must be verified at intake and reviewed at each classification stage.

6.4. Metadata errors must be corrected through a documented correction process. Correction requests must be submitted in writing and recorded in the work's governance file. The correction process follows the decision and record requirements in AIWA-GOV-02.

6.5. AIWA must not publish, license, or distribute a work with known metadata errors.

6.6. Metadata submitted by the rights holder does not override AIWA's verification obligation. AIWA must independently verify metadata claims against available documentary evidence.

---

## 7. AI-Assisted Works

7.1. Works created with material AI assistance require separate verification procedures.

7.2. For AI-assisted works, verification must establish:

- which elements of the work were created by a human author;
- which elements were generated by an AI system;
- the rights holder's ownership of the human-authored elements;
- the terms under which the AI system was used; and
- whether the AI system's use creates any third-party rights claims.

7.3. The human-authored elements of an AI-assisted work are subject to standard ownership verification.

7.4. The AI-generated elements of an AI-assisted work are not independently owned by AIWA and are not independently eligible for rights administration unless a recognized rights holder exists for those elements.

7.5. AI-assisted works must be classified in accordance with AIWA-DOC-01.

---

## 8. Community-Origin Materials

8.1. Works submitted from a community context — including community archives, oral history projects, and collaborative cultural works — require community provenance verification.

8.2. Community provenance verification must establish:

- the community of origin;
- the authority of the submitting party to submit on the community's behalf;
- whether the material includes any TCEs subject to AIWA-POL-03;
- whether any consent from community elders, cultural authorities, or governance structures is required; and
- the applicable cultural restrictions on the material.

8.3. AIWA must not accept a community-origin submission without documented authority from an appropriate community representative.

8.4. Community-origin materials with unclear authority must be Held pending consultation with the Cultural Review Unit.

---

## 9. Documentary Evidence Standards

9.1. The following documents are acceptable as primary evidence of ownership or provenance:

- original creation agreements;
- copyright registration certificates;
- publishing agreements;
- assignment or transfer agreements;
- succession documents;
- community authority letters on official letterhead; and
- government-issued certificates of cultural ownership or designation.

9.2. The following documents support but do not independently establish ownership:

- creator affidavits;
- submission acknowledgment records;
- bank or payment records showing royalty payments;
- social media or publication metadata; and
- witness statements.

9.3. No verbal claim of ownership constitutes acceptable evidence.

9.4. AIWA must record the documentary basis for each ownership verification decision.

9.5. Documents must be in a language readable by the reviewing officer or accompanied by a certified translation.

---

## 10. Version Control

10.1. Where a work exists in multiple versions, each version is treated as a separate item for rights verification purposes unless the rights holder provides documentation confirming that all versions are covered by a single rights instrument.

10.2. AIWA must maintain version records including:

- version identifier;
- version creation date;
- modifications from the prior version;
- rights holder for the version; and
- any new rights or restrictions introduced in the version.

10.3. A new or substantially modified version of a work must be verified before licensing or processing separately from prior versions.

---

## 11. Rights Revocation Procedures

11.1. A rights holder may revoke AIWA's administration authority for a work by providing written notice to AIWA.

11.2. Upon receipt of a revocation notice, AIWA must:

- confirm receipt in writing;
- remove the work from active licensing and exploitation;
- notify active sublicensees of the revocation;
- wind down royalty collection in an orderly manner;
- transfer all royalty accounting records to the rights holder; and
- complete the revocation process within the timeframe specified in the creator agreement.

11.3. Revocation does not extinguish royalties already earned for the period before revocation.

11.4. Revocation does not automatically terminate existing sublicenses. AIWA must communicate revocation to sublicensees and manage wind-down in accordance with the terms of those sublicenses.

11.5. Where revocation is disputed, the matter is escalated to the Governance Board.

---

## 12. Fraud and Misrepresentation

12.1. Submitting false ownership information to AIWA is a material breach of the creator agreement.

12.2. AIWA must Reject or Restrict any submission where fraudulent ownership documentation is identified.

12.3. AIWA must notify relevant authorities where fraudulent submissions involve significant works or where a pattern of fraud is identified.

12.4. Verified fraud in a rights submission results in:

- immediate removal of the work from the AIWA system;
- escrow of all royalties associated with the work pending investigation;
- notification to all active sublicensees; and
- referral to the Governance Board for determination.

## 13. Contributor Warranties and Representations

13.1. By submitting any Work to the AIWA system, the contributor represents and warrants that they own or possess all rights, licences, permissions, consents, and authority necessary to submit, register, administer, distribute, licence, stream, computationally authorize, and otherwise make available the contributed Work and all associated materials through the AIWA platform and any sublicensed channels.

13.2. The contributor's warranty of authority applies to all rights categories relevant to the intended use of the Work, including without limitation:

- reproduction rights;
- distribution rights;
- communication and streaming rights;
- adaptation and translation rights where applicable;
- synchronization rights where applicable;
- performance rights where applicable;
- publishing rights where applicable;
- all rights necessary for educational processing, archival, and licensed AI-related functions under AIWA-POL-04; and
- any other rights required for the authorized uses permitted under the creator agreement or applicable licence.

13.3. Submission constitutes an affirmative, binding representation of the contributor's ownership or authorized authority. This warranty is a condition of admission to the AIWA system and continues for the duration of AIWA's administration of the Work.

13.4. Where the contributor is not the original creator, the contributor must warrant that they have obtained the original creator's express written authority to submit the Work and to grant AIWA the administrative authority provided for in the creator agreement.

---

## 14. Third-Party Rights Responsibility

14.1. Contributors are solely responsible for securing all required permissions, licences, releases, clearances, synchronization rights, performance rights, publishing rights, image rights, and other third-party authorizations associated with submitted Works before submission to AIWA.

14.2. Third-party rights obligations include, without limitation:

- clearances for samples, interpolations, or incorporated third-party musical compositions or recordings;
- synchronization licences for Works incorporating pre-existing recordings;
- image and likeness rights for Works containing identifiable persons;
- clearances for adapted or translated Works derived from third-party sources;
- publishing rights for Works containing licensed literary, textual, or compositional content;
- all collective society, PRO, or mechanical licensing clearances required for the relevant jurisdictions;
- consents required under applicable data protection, privacy, or cultural heritage laws; and
- any community authority or cultural institution permissions required for Works with traditional cultural expressions or community origin.

14.3. AIWA does not provide legal clearance services and does not independently verify third-party clearances obtained by contributors. The contributor bears sole legal responsibility for the validity and completeness of their submission with respect to third-party rights.

14.4. Where a third-party rights claim is made against a Work in the AIWA system, AIWA may classify the Work as Held or Restricted pending the contributor's resolution of the claim. AIWA is not obligated to defend, indemnify, or contribute to the cost of resolving third-party rights claims arising from contributor submissions.

14.5. AIWA may require the contributor to provide documentary evidence of third-party clearances before activating licensing or distribution for Works where third-party rights are apparent or likely.

---

## 15. Chain-of-Title Documentation Requirement

15.1. For Works submitted for commercial exploitation, licensing, or distribution, the contributor must be able to demonstrate an unbroken chain of title from the original creator to the submitting party.

15.2. Acceptable chain-of-title documentation includes:

- original creation agreements confirming authorship;
- assignment or rights transfer agreements;
- work-for-hire agreements establishing employer or commissioning party ownership;
- co-authorship or collaboration agreements defining each party's rights and share;
- testamentary, succession, or inheritance documents;
- licence-back agreements where publishing or related rights have been assigned and re-licenced; and
- any other documents establishing an unbroken rights chain recognized under applicable law.

15.3. AIWA may request chain-of-title documentation at any point in the administration lifecycle, including after initial admission, if a dispute, gap, or inconsistency is identified.

15.4. Inability to provide adequate chain-of-title documentation upon request is grounds for suspension of licensing and distribution activity for the affected Work until documentation is provided or the matter is resolved by the Governance Board.

15.5. Where AIWA acts in reliance upon contributor representations and subsequently suffers a claim, penalty, loss, or damage arising from inaccurate, incomplete, or fraudulent chain-of-title documentation, the contributor is liable to indemnify AIWA for all such claims, costs, expenses, and damages, to the extent permitted under applicable law and the creator agreement.

15.6. Acceptance of a Work by AIWA does not constitute AIWA's validation of the contributor's chain of title. Ongoing chain-of-title responsibility remains with the contributor for the duration of AIWA's administration of the Work.
