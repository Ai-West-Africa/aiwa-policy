# Rights Administration & Licensing Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-01
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 1 — Foundational Governance
**Scope:** AIWA's role, authority, and obligations as a collective rights administration and licensing organization.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-DOC-02 (Legal Alignment), AIWA-POL-02 (Royalty Governance), AIWA-POL-05 (Rights Verification), AIWA-GOV-01 (Roles), AIWA-GOV-02 (Decision Framework)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines AIWA's role as a collective rights administration and licensing organization.

1.2. This document defines the scope of rights AIWA administers, the authority under which AIWA acts, and the boundaries of that authority.

1.3. This document defines the non-ownership principle that governs all AIWA operations.

1.4. This document is binding across all AIWA policies, agreements, and operational decisions.

---

## 2. Organizational Role

2.1. AIWA is a multi-format collective rights administration and licensing organization.

2.2. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders. AIWA does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

2.3. AIWA operates as:

- a non-exclusive rights administrator;
- a licensing coordination organization;
- a collective rights management platform;
- a royalty collection and distribution body; and
- a cultural stewardship and governance organization.

2.4. AIWA does not operate as:

- an owner of creative works;
- a publisher by default;
- an extractor of cultural assets;
- an AI data aggregator; or
- a purchaser of cultural identity.

2.5. AIWA operates as a multi-format rights administration, licensing coordination, and cultural stewardship organization covering music, audiovisual works, books, educational content, podcasts, radio, spoken word, AI-processable materials, metadata, developmental works, and cultural-language content.

---

## 3. Non-Ownership Declaration

3.1. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

3.2. This declaration applies to all work types in the AIWA system without exception.

3.3. Administration authority does not equal ownership.

3.4. Licensing authority does not equal ownership.

3.5. Sublicensing authority does not equal ownership.

3.6. Royalty collection authority does not equal ownership.

3.7. No AIWA agreement, action, or decision may be interpreted as a transfer of ownership unless ownership transfer is expressly stated in a separately executed written agreement.

---

## 4. Rights Categories

4.1. AIWA administers rights across the following categories:

| Rights Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Music Rights | Composition rights, performance rights, synchronization rights, master recording rights |
| Literary Rights | Books, written works, manuscripts, scripts, screenplays |
| Audiovisual Rights | Film, video, documentary, television, digital video |
| Educational Rights | Textbooks, curricula, instructional materials, educational recordings |
| Developmental Materials | Drafts, concepts, treatments, outlines, pilot episodes, pitch materials |
| Metadata Rights | Descriptive metadata, structural metadata, provenance metadata |
| AI-Processable Materials | Works cleared for educational processing, linguistic analysis, and dataset use |
| Cultural-Language Content | Works in Gambian and West African languages; oral traditions in authored form |
| Spoken Word & Podcast | Podcasts, audio recordings, spoken word performances |
| Radio Content | Broadcast radio, archived radio recordings, produced radio content |

4.2. Rights administration within each category is governed by the applicable classification under AIWA-DOC-01 and AIWA-STD-03.

4.3. Rights categories are not mutually exclusive. A single work may fall under more than one rights category.

---

## 5. Non-Exclusive Administration

5.1. AIWA's administration of rights is non-exclusive unless a separate written agreement expressly states otherwise.

5.2. Under non-exclusive administration:

- the creator retains ownership of the work;
- the creator may self-license the work;
- the creator may work with other administrators or distributors; and
- the creator may withdraw from AIWA administration subject to the terms of their agreement.

5.3. AIWA may not restrict a creator from entering separate licensing arrangements unless a specific exclusivity agreement has been executed in writing by both parties.

5.4. Non-exclusive administration is the default position. Exclusivity must be expressly stated, separately justified, and documented.

---

## 6. Sublicensing Authority

6.1. AIWA holds sublicensing authority for works in the AIWA system subject to:

- the terms of the relevant creator agreement;
- applicable classification decisions under AIWA-DOC-01; and
- the cultural and legal constraints set out in AIWA-DOC-02.

6.2. AIWA may appoint or authorize the following classes of sublicensees:

- U.S.-based rights administration entities;
- digital service providers (DSPs) including streaming platforms;
- AI processing and dataset partners;
- educational publishers and academic platforms;
- broadcasters and radio licensing bodies;
- synchronization agencies;
- archival and preservation institutions; and
- foreign sublicensing agents operating within approved territories.

6.3. All sublicensing arrangements must:

- be documented in writing;
- be consistent with the creator's agreement with AIWA;
- not exceed the rights AIWA holds under the creator agreement;
- preserve the creator's ownership; and
- be terminable where the underlying creator agreement is terminated.

6.4. AIWA must not grant sublicenses for uses that have been restricted, held, or rejected under the classification and decision system.

6.5. Sublicensees must comply with AIWA's cultural governance requirements. AIWA must include cultural governance obligations in sublicensing agreements for works with cultural or heritage classification.

---

## 7. Royalty Collection and Distribution

7.1. AIWA collects royalties on behalf of rights holders from all licensed uses of works in the AIWA system.

7.2. Revenue sources include:

- DSP streaming and download royalties;
- user-generated content (UGC) platform payments;
- synchronization licensing fees;
- AI processing and dataset licensing fees;
- educational licensing fees;
- archival and preservation licensing fees;
- foreign sublicensing revenues; and
- broadcast and radio performance royalties.

7.3. All royalties collected by AIWA on behalf of rights holders belong to those rights holders, subject to AIWA's administration fees and applicable deductions.

7.4. AIWA must maintain accurate accounting records for all royalties collected and distributed.

7.5. Royalty distribution is governed by AIWA-POL-02.

---

## 8. Enforcement Authority

8.1. AIWA holds enforcement authority for works in the AIWA system.

8.2. Enforcement authority includes:

- issuing takedown notices for unauthorized use;
- filing DMCA-style notices with digital platforms;
- coordinating with fingerprinting and content identification systems;
- pursuing anti-piracy actions in applicable jurisdictions; and
- initiating claims for unauthorized exploitation of administered works.

8.3. Enforcement authority is exercised on behalf of rights holders. Enforcement actions do not transfer ownership to AIWA.

8.4. Copyright enforcement procedures are governed by AIWA-POL-07.

---

## 9. Reservation of Ownership

9.1. All rights in a work remain with the rights holder unless a separate written agreement expressly transfers those rights.

9.2. Administration by AIWA does not constitute an assignment of rights.

9.3. Licensing by AIWA does not constitute an assignment of rights.

9.4. Sublicensing by AIWA does not constitute an assignment of rights.

9.5. Royalty collection by AIWA does not constitute an assignment of rights.

9.6. AIWA's administration terminates when the relevant creator agreement terminates.

9.7. Upon termination of administration, AIWA must:

- cease licensing activity for the work;
- transfer accounting records to the rights holder;
- communicate termination to active sublicensees; and
- wind down royalty collection for the work in an orderly manner.

---

## 10. No Obligation to Exploit

10.1. AIWA holds no obligation to actively exploit, license, or commercialize any specific work in the AIWA system.

10.2. Admission of a work into the AIWA system does not create a guarantee of:

- licensing placement;
- royalty earnings;
- distribution activity; or
- platform placement.

10.3. AIWA makes reasonable commercial efforts on behalf of rights holders but does not warrant specific outcomes.

10.4. Rights holders may not claim damages based solely on AIWA's failure to exploit a work, unless a specific exploitation commitment is made in a separately executed written agreement.

---

## 11. Technical Use Rights

11.1. AIWA holds the following technical use rights for works in the AIWA system for the purpose of operating the administration and licensing infrastructure:

- file compression and format conversion;
- transcoding for platform delivery;
- translation of metadata for international licensing;
- fingerprinting for content identification and enforcement;
- metadata transformation for rights management systems;
- educational processing and linguistic analysis, subject to classification; and
- archival duplication for preservation purposes.

11.2. Technical use rights are limited to the operational purpose stated.

11.3. Technical use rights do not constitute exploitation rights.

11.4. Technical use rights do not override cultural classification restrictions.

11.5. Works classified as folklore, sacred, or restricted may not be subjected to AI-processing technical use without separate authorization.

---

## 12. Governance and Compliance

12.1. This policy operates within the legal hierarchy established in AIWA-DOC-02.

12.2. Gambian law governs all intake, classification, cultural handling, and payout decisions.

12.3. No commercial agreement may override the cultural and legal protections established in AIWA-DOC-02.

12.4. Rights administration activities that touch works with cultural or heritage classification must be reviewed by the Cultural Review Unit before execution.

12.5. Disputes arising under this policy are resolved in accordance with AIWA-GOV-02.

---

## 13. Core Statements

13.1. AIWA administers rights. AIWA does not own rights.

13.2. AIWA licenses on behalf of creators. AIWA does not extinguish creator ownership.

13.3. AIWA collects royalties for creators. AIWA does not retain royalties beyond authorized administration fees.

13.4. AIWA enforces on behalf of creators. AIWA does not enforce for its own benefit.

13.5. AIWA's authority derives from creator agreements. AIWA may not exceed the authority granted in those agreements.
