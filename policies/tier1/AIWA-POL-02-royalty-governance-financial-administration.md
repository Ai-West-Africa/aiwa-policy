# Royalty Governance & Financial Administration Policy

**Document ID:** AIWA-POL-02
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Tier:** 1 — Foundational Governance
**Scope:** Royalty collection, distribution, escrow, financial governance, and dispute resolution for all works in the AIWA system.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow. Those are contained in AIWA-DOC-03.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-POL-01 (Rights Administration), AIWA-DOC-02 (Legal Alignment), AIWA-GOV-01 (Roles), AIWA-GOV-02 (Decision Framework)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines AIWA's royalty governance and financial administration framework.

1.2. This document establishes the standards for revenue collection, escrow, distribution, dispute resolution, and audit transparency.

1.3. This document is a trust instrument. It governs how AIWA handles money that belongs to rights holders.

1.4. AIWA administers royalties on behalf of rights holders. Collected royalties belong to the rights holders, not to AIWA.

1.5. AIWA administers rights on behalf of rights holders and does not acquire ownership of copyrights, communal heritage, languages, or cultural identity except where expressly agreed in writing.

---

## 2. Revenue Sources

2.1. AIWA collects royalties and licensing revenues from the following source categories:

| Source Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| DSP Streaming | Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music, Audiomack, Boomplay |
| DSP Downloads | Platform download fees |
| User-Generated Content (UGC) | YouTube Content ID, TikTok, Meta |
| Synchronization | Film, TV, advertising, game placements |
| AI Processing Licensing | Dataset licensing fees from authorized AI partners |
| Educational Licensing | Academic publishers, institutional educational platforms |
| Archival Licensing | Library systems, preservation institutions |
| Foreign Sublicensing | Revenues from sublicensing agents in non-primary territories |
| Broadcast & Radio | Broadcast performance royalties, radio play royalties |
| Spoken Word & Podcast | Platform monetization, licensing fees |

2.2. Revenue source classification determines the applicable distribution rate and timeline.

2.3. AIWA must maintain a current register of active revenue sources and applicable collection frameworks.

---

## 3. Collection Methodology

3.1. AIWA collects revenues from authorized platforms, sublicensing agents, and licensing partners.

3.2. AIWA must verify that every revenue stream is traceable to an active, classified, and approved work in the AIWA system.

3.3. Revenue received for an unverified or unclassified work must be held in escrow pending verification.

3.4. AIWA must record the following for every revenue receipt:

- source platform or partner;
- work identifier;
- rights holder identifier;
- period covered;
- gross amount received;
- applicable deductions; and
- net amount attributable to the rights holder.

3.5. Collection records must be retained for a minimum of seven years.

---

## 4. Escrow Rules

4.1. AIWA must hold the following funds in escrow and must not distribute them until the escrow condition is resolved:

| Escrow Condition | Resolution Requirement |
|---|---|
| Work is unverified or unclassified | Verification and classification must be completed |
| Rights holder identity is unconfirmed | Identity verification must be completed |
| Ownership dispute is active | Dispute must be resolved under Section 12 |
| Work is under active legal hold | Legal hold must be lifted by the issuing authority |
| Fraud investigation is open | Investigation must be concluded |
| Currency conversion is pending | Conversion must be executed and reconciled |

4.2. Escrow funds must be held in a segregated account. Escrow funds must not be commingled with AIWA operating funds.

4.3. AIWA must provide rights holders with a statement of escrowed amounts and the applicable escrow condition upon request.

4.4. Escrow must not be used as a mechanism to delay legitimate payments.

---

## 5. Fraud Reserves

5.1. AIWA must maintain a fraud reserve fund funded by a percentage of gross revenues, as determined by the Governance Board.

5.2. The fraud reserve exists to:

- cover chargebacks and clawbacks from platforms; and
- absorb losses from fraudulent streaming, false attribution, and misrepresented rights.

5.3. The fraud reserve percentage must be reviewed by the Governance Board no less than annually.

5.4. Fraud reserve funds must not be distributed to rights holders until a fraud investigation is concluded and the reserve need is assessed.

5.5. Rights holders who are confirmed fraud perpetrators forfeit all rights to accumulated royalties from fraudulent activity.

---

## 6. Currency Conversion

6.1. Royalties received in foreign currencies must be converted to the applicable distribution currency.

6.2. AIWA must document the conversion rate applied to each transaction.

6.3. AIWA must apply a commercially reasonable conversion rate at the time of conversion.

6.4. AIWA must disclose the conversion methodology to rights holders upon request.

6.5. Currency conversion losses must not be passed to rights holders in excess of actual conversion costs.

6.6. AIWA must maintain records of all currency conversion transactions for a minimum of seven years.

---

## 7. Dormant Royalties

7.1. Royalties are dormant when:

- the rights holder cannot be located;
- the rights holder has not claimed the royalties within the period defined in their agreement; or
- distribution instructions are missing or incomplete.

7.2. Dormant royalties must remain in escrow for a minimum of three years from the point of dormancy.

7.3. During the dormancy period, AIWA must make reasonable efforts to locate the rights holder.

7.4. After the dormancy period, AIWA may transfer dormant royalties to a designated community benefit fund, subject to Governance Board approval and applicable law.

7.5. AIWA must maintain a public dormant royalties register listing dormant cases without personal data.

---

## 8. Unclaimed Royalties

8.1. Unclaimed royalties arise when a rights holder has been identified but has not submitted a valid claim.

8.2. AIWA must notify rights holders of unclaimed royalties no less than annually.

8.3. Unclaimed royalties must be held in escrow for no less than three years from the date of the first notification.

8.4. AIWA must maintain a complete record of all notifications sent for unclaimed royalties.

8.5. After the applicable holding period, unclaimed royalties are subject to the dormant royalties process under Section 7.

---

## 9. Estate and Beneficiary Procedures

9.1. When a rights holder dies, royalty collection continues for all active administered works.

9.2. AIWA must hold royalties for a deceased rights holder in escrow pending:

- confirmation of the estate representative; and
- receipt of the applicable legal documentation.

9.3. AIWA must require the following documentation before distributing royalties to an estate:

- death certificate or equivalent official document;
- legal authorization confirming the estate representative's authority; and
- confirmation of the work's continued inclusion in the estate.

9.4. AIWA must not distribute royalties to a claimed beneficiary without verified legal documentation.

9.5. Disputed estate claims must be escalated to the Governance Board.

9.6. AIWA does not adjudicate estate disputes. Contested claims must be resolved by a court or applicable legal authority.

---

## 10. Audit and Transparency Standards

10.1. AIWA must conduct an internal financial audit no less than annually.

10.2. AIWA must retain an independent external auditor to review royalty accounting no less than every two years.

10.3. Audit findings must be reported to the Governance Board.

10.4. Rights holders must receive a minimum of one royalty statement per year.

10.5. Royalty statements must include:

- total revenues collected in the period;
- applicable deductions and fees;
- net amount due; and
- amount paid and payment date.

10.6. Rights holders have the right to request an account review for any royalty statement within twelve months of receipt.

10.7. AIWA must respond to account review requests within sixty days.

---

## 11. Chargebacks and Platform Clawbacks

11.1. AIWA must maintain a clawback reserve in addition to the fraud reserve to manage platform chargebacks.

11.2. A platform clawback occurs when a DSP or platform reverses previously paid royalties.

11.3. When a clawback is received, AIWA must:

- verify the platform's basis for the clawback;
- dispute the clawback if the basis is not valid;
- deduct the clawback from the applicable rights holder account if the basis is valid; and
- notify the rights holder of the deduction.

11.4. Rights holders must not be deducted for clawbacks that result from AIWA's administrative error.

11.5. All clawback events must be documented and retained for a minimum of seven years.

---

## 12. Dispute Resolution

12.1. A royalty dispute arises when a rights holder disputes the amount, calculation, or withholding of a royalty payment.

12.2. Rights holders must submit disputes in writing to AIWA within twelve months of the relevant royalty statement.

12.3. AIWA must acknowledge receipt of a dispute within ten business days.

12.4. AIWA must investigate and provide a written response within sixty days of acknowledgment.

12.5. If the dispute cannot be resolved by AIWA's internal process, the matter is escalated to the Governance Board.

12.6. All dispute records must be retained for a minimum of seven years.

12.7. Disputed amounts must be held in escrow during the dispute period. AIWA must not distribute disputed amounts to any party until the dispute is resolved.

---

## 13. Administration Fees

13.1. AIWA deducts an administration fee from royalties collected on behalf of rights holders.

13.2. The administration fee percentage must be:

- established by the Governance Board;
- documented in every creator agreement;
- disclosed to rights holders before execution of the creator agreement; and
- reviewed no less than every three years.

13.3. Administration fees must not exceed the maximum permitted by the creator agreement.

13.4. AIWA must not impose undisclosed fees.

---

## 14. Core Statements

14.1. Royalties collected by AIWA belong to rights holders.

14.2. AIWA holds royalties in trust until distribution.

14.3. Escrowed funds are not AIWA operating funds.

14.4. AIWA's financial administration is subject to audit.

14.5. No rights holder forfeits royalties without written notice and due process.
