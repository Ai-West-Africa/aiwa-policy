## 3. Allowed Decision States

3.1. The only allowed decision states are:

- **Approved** — the work is cleared for the stated scope of use.
- **Approved with Limits** — the work is cleared with defined restrictions on scope, territory, or use type.
- **Held** — the work is paused pending further information or review.
- **Restricted** — the work is blocked from the stated use due to classification or legal grounds.
- **Rejected** — the work does not meet the requirements for entry into the system.
- **Escalated** — the decision has been referred to the Governance Board.

3.2. No additional decision states are permitted.
3.3. Every decision must be recorded with a decision state.
3.4. No undocumented decisions are permitted.
3.5. No verbal approvals are permitted.

## Authoring Instructions

The following instructions apply when creating a new procedure document using this template.

**Formatting:**
- Use numbered sections: 1, 1.1, 1.2.
- Use short, direct sentences.
- Use stage-based structure. Each stage is a separate section.

**Each Stage Must Include:**
- What — what happens.
- Why — why it is required.
- Who — which roles are involved.
- When — when the stage is triggered.
- Where — in which system or location.
- How — step-by-step numbered actions.
- Output — the output and status change.

**Stages Must Not Be Merged.**

**Language:**
- Use: "must", "must not", "only", "does not".
- Do not use: "should" (unless explicitly required), "generally", "typically".

**Decision States (Locked):**
Only the following states are permitted: Approved, Approved with Limits, Held, Restricted, Rejected, Escalated.
Do not introduce additional states.

**Control Rules:**
Every procedure document must include a Control Rules section.
Every procedure document must include an Escalation Rules section.
