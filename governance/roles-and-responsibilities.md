# Roles and Responsibilities

**Document ID:** AIWA-GOV-01
**Version:** 1.0
**Scope:** Definitions of all roles and their responsibilities within the AIWA governance system.

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines all roles within the AIWA governance system.
1.2. This document defines the responsibilities of each role.
1.3. This document defines the authority boundaries of each role.

---

## 2. AIWA Governance Structure

2.1. AIWA is the mandatory local steward and governance authority for all works in this system.
2.2. The governance structure consists of the following roles:

- Governance Board
- Intake Officer
- Classification Officer
- Legal Officer
- Stewardship Officer
- PRIME Liaison

2.3. No role may exercise authority beyond the scope defined in this document.
2.4. No role may approve, release, or exploit a work outside the process defined in AIWA-DOC-03.

---

## 3. Governance Board

### 3.1. Role Definition
The Governance Board is the final decision-making authority within the AIWA system.

### 3.2. Responsibilities
3.2.1. The Governance Board resolves all escalated decisions.
3.2.2. The Governance Board issues final decisions in one of the allowed decision states.
3.2.3. The Governance Board maintains oversight of all roles within this system.
3.2.4. The Governance Board reviews and approves updates to AIWA policy documents.

### 3.3. Authority Boundaries
3.3.1. The Governance Board must record all decisions in writing.
3.3.2. No Governance Board decision may override Gambian law.
3.3.3. No Governance Board decision may be issued verbally.

### 3.4. Composition
3.4.1. The Governance Board must include representation from AIWA ownership.
3.4.2. No single actor holds majority control over the Governance Board.

---

## 4. Intake Officer

### 4.1. Role Definition
The Intake Officer is responsible for receiving and logging all works submitted to the AIWA system.

### 4.2. Responsibilities
4.2.1. The Intake Officer receives submissions from Submitting Parties.
4.2.2. The Intake Officer assigns unique submission reference numbers.
4.2.3. The Intake Officer validates the completeness of each submission.
4.2.4. The Intake Officer logs all submission details in the AIWA intake record.
4.2.5. The Intake Officer passes complete submissions to the Classification Officer.
4.2.6. The Intake Officer returns incomplete submissions with a written explanation.

### 4.3. Authority Boundaries
4.3.1. The Intake Officer does not make classification decisions.
4.3.2. The Intake Officer does not make legal determinations.
4.3.3. The Intake Officer does not approve or restrict works.

---

## 5. Classification Officer

### 5.1. Role Definition
The Classification Officer is responsible for assigning and recording primary classification decisions.

### 5.2. Responsibilities
5.2.1. The Classification Officer applies the classification logic defined in AIWA-DOC-01.
5.2.2. The Classification Officer records all classification decisions with a written rationale.
5.2.3. The Classification Officer flags AI Training Inputs for additional control review.
5.2.4. The Classification Officer flags Folklore and Folklore-Linked works for restricted status.
5.2.5. The Classification Officer escalates ambiguous classifications to the Governance Board.
5.2.6. The Classification Officer consults the Legal Officer when classification has legal implications.

### 5.3. Authority Boundaries
5.3.1. The Classification Officer does not approve works for exploitation.
5.3.2. The Classification Officer does not make final decisions on escalated matters.
5.3.3. The Classification Officer must not reclassify a work without a documented decision.

---

## 6. Legal Officer

### 6.1. Role Definition
The Legal Officer is responsible for confirming legal compliance and ministry alignment for all works.

### 6.2. Responsibilities
6.2.1. The Legal Officer identifies applicable Gambian laws for each work type and classification.
6.2.2. The Legal Officer identifies all ministries with applicable jurisdiction.
6.2.3. The Legal Officer verifies the scope and validity of government authorizations.
6.2.4. The Legal Officer records legal clearances and ministry identifications.
6.2.5. The Legal Officer escalates matters with unclear or disputed jurisdiction to the Governance Board.
6.2.6. The Legal Officer sets the decision state when works fail legal review.

### 6.3. Authority Boundaries
6.3.1. The Legal Officer does not assign classifications.
6.3.2. The Legal Officer does not manage payout.
6.3.3. The Legal Officer does not negotiate with PRIME.

---

## 7. Stewardship Officer

### 7.1. Role Definition
The Stewardship Officer is responsible for managing ongoing rights, records, and payout for all registered works.

### 7.2. Responsibilities
7.2.1. The Stewardship Officer records AIWA as local steward in the stewardship registry.
7.2.2. The Stewardship Officer records creator ownership and payout terms.
7.2.3. The Stewardship Officer maintains up-to-date records for each work.
7.2.4. The Stewardship Officer records all exploitation events.
7.2.5. The Stewardship Officer calculates and executes payout under agreed terms.
7.2.6. The Stewardship Officer records each payout with a reference to the triggering exploitation event.

### 7.3. Authority Boundaries
7.3.1. The Stewardship Officer does not assign classifications.
7.3.2. The Stewardship Officer does not make legal determinations.
7.3.3. The Stewardship Officer must not execute payout without a corresponding exploitation record.

---

## 8. PRIME Liaison

### 8.1. Role Definition
The PRIME Liaison manages all communication and referrals between AIWA and PRIME for offshore exploitation.

### 8.2. Responsibilities
8.2.1. The PRIME Liaison confirms a work's approval status before any referral to PRIME.
8.2.2. The PRIME Liaison prepares referral records including classification, approved scope, and restrictions.
8.2.3. The PRIME Liaison transmits referrals to PRIME through the designated contractual channel.
8.2.4. The PRIME Liaison reports offshore exploitation activity to the Stewardship Officer.

### 8.3. Authority Boundaries
8.3.1. The PRIME Liaison must not refer a work to PRIME without AIWA approval on record.
8.3.2. The PRIME Liaison must not authorize PRIME to exceed the approved scope.
8.3.3. The PRIME Liaison must not communicate a reclassification or restriction removal to PRIME.

---

## 9. Submitting Party

### 9.1. Role Definition
The Submitting Party is the creator, rights holder, or authorized representative who submits a work to the AIWA system.

### 9.2. Responsibilities
9.2.1. The Submitting Party provides the work and all required documentation at Stage 1.
9.2.2. The Submitting Party confirms ownership details at Stage 4.
9.2.3. The Submitting Party receives payout under agreed terms.

### 9.3. Authority Boundaries
9.3.1. The Submitting Party does not make classification decisions.
9.3.2. The Submitting Party does not determine payout amounts independently.
9.3.3. The Submitting Party may dispute a classification decision. Disputes must be submitted in writing and escalated to the Governance Board.

---

## 10. PRIME

### 10.1. Role Definition
PRIME is the non-exclusive offshore rights and exploitation partner.

### 10.2. Responsibilities
10.2.1. PRIME exploits works referred to it by AIWA within the approved scope defined in the referral record.
10.2.2. PRIME reports exploitation activity to the PRIME Liaison.

### 10.3. Authority Boundaries
10.3.1. PRIME must not exploit a work that AIWA has not approved.
10.3.2. PRIME must not exceed the approved scope defined in the referral record.
10.3.3. PRIME must not reclassify a work.
10.3.4. PRIME must not remove or override any restriction applied by AIWA.
10.3.5. PRIME does not hold local stewardship.
10.3.6. PRIME does not hold classification authority.
