# Roles and Responsibilities

**Document ID:** AIWA-GOV-01
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Scope:** Definitions of all roles and their responsibilities within the AIWA governance system.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-DOC-03 (Process & Procedures), AIWA-GOV-02 (Decision Framework)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines every role within the AIWA governance and operations system.

1.2. For each role, this document defines:

- the role definition;
- the responsibilities assigned to that role; and
- the authority boundaries of that role.

1.3. No role may exceed its stated authority boundaries. Actions taken outside authority boundaries are not valid AIWA decisions.

---

## 2. AIWA Governance Structure

2.1. The AIWA governance structure consists of the following layers:

| Layer | Function |
|---|---|
| Governance Board | Highest internal authority; handles escalated matters and system-level decisions |
| Legal / Compliance Unit | Manages legal review, government authorizations, and compliance |
| Cultural Review Unit | Manages folklore, sacred, and community-linked review |
| Rights Administration | Verifies submission authority and rights status |
| Classification Officer | Assigns and confirms classification categories |
| Decision Officer | Issues formal decisions at the decision gate |
| Intake Staff | Receives submissions and creates intake records |
| Monitoring and Compliance Team | Monitors approved works post-release |
| Appeals Officer | Manages appeals and corrections |
| Quality and Governance Unit | Manages audit, metrics, and process improvement |
| PRIME Liaison | Manages the AIWA-PRIME operating relationship |
| Submitting Party | External contributor who submits works to AIWA |

2.2. Each layer is accountable to the layer above it. No layer may override the Governance Board.

## 3. Governance Board

### 3.1. Role Definition

The Governance Board is the highest internal decision-making authority within AIWA. It handles escalated, high-stakes, and unresolved matters that cannot be closed at operational level.

### 3.2. Responsibilities

- Adjudicate all escalated cases.
- Set system-level policy for classification, rights, and cultural handling.
- Oversee amendments to AIWA policy documents.
- Review audit reports from the Quality and Governance Unit.
- Make final decisions on folklore determination disputes.
- Confirm decisions involving government-authorized restricted works where escalation has occurred.
- Define the fixed schedule for Stage 13 audits.

### 3.3. Authority Boundaries

- The Governance Board is the final internal authority for escalated matters.
- The Governance Board does not override Gambian law or applicable ministerial authority.
- The Governance Board does not have authority to unilaterally reclassify a work as non-folklore if the evidence supports a folklore classification.
- Decisions of the Governance Board must be documented.

### 3.4. Composition

- Composition is determined by AIWA's constitutional or founding documents.
- A high-risk case must not be closed by a single person. Board decisions on high-stakes matters require more than one member.

### 3.5. External Advisory and Public-Interest Interfaces

- The Governance Board may request non-voting input from relevant ministries, NCAC-linked authorities, academic or technical experts, community representatives, or independent public-interest reviewers where a matter raises cultural, educational, research, or public-authority sensitivities.
- External advisory participants are consultative. They do not issue final AIWA decisions unless they are separately acting under recognized legal authority or under another policy-authorized role.
- Where independent ethics or public-interest review is requested, the Governance Board must document the review scope, the basis for selecting the reviewer, and any conflict-of-interest controls applied.

## 4. Intake Staff

### 4.1. Role Definition

Intake Staff are the first point of contact for all submitted works. They receive submissions and create the formal intake record.

### 4.2. Responsibilities

- Receive all submitted works through authorized intake channels.
- Create the intake record for each submission, capturing all required fields as defined in AIWA-DOC-03 Stage 1.
- Confirm that all five Confirmational Statements have been affirmed by the contributor before proceeding.
- Assign the Intake ID.
- Pass the intake record to the next stage.

### 4.3. Authority Boundaries

- Intake Staff do not classify works.
- Intake Staff do not make decisions on rights, restrictions, or AI eligibility.
- Intake Staff may return incomplete submissions to the contributor.
- Intake Staff may not approve or reject any work.

---

## 5. Classification Officer

### 5.1. Role Definition

The Classification Officer is responsible for assigning and confirming the formal classification category of submitted works, using the categories and rules in AIWA-DOC-01.

### 5.2. Responsibilities

- Conduct the Initial Risk Screen (Stage 4) and assign a risk level.
- Assign the Initial Classification (Stage 5) using the five classification categories defined in AIWA-DOC-01 Section 6.
- Assign a confidence level (High, Medium, Low) alongside each classification.
- Flag works for elevated or restricted-risk handling where required.
- Mark works as "Requires Cultural Review Context" where controlled ambiguity exists.
- Escalate to the Cultural Review Unit where the work is elevated beyond standard handling.

### 5.3. Authority Boundaries

- The Classification Officer assigns the initial classification. Final classification authority on contested or escalated cases rests with the Cultural Review Unit or Governance Board.
- The Classification Officer may not override the locked classification logic in AIWA-DOC-01 Section 5.1.
- The Classification Officer may not approve or reject a work.

---

## 6. Rights Administrator

### 6.1. Role Definition

The Rights Administrator verifies whether the submitting party has the authority to place the work in the AIWA system and determines the rights status of the work.

### 6.2. Responsibilities

- Conduct the Rights and Authority Check (Stage 6).
- Verify contributor authority to submit.
- Verify co-author status and required co-author consents.
- Verify school or guardian permissions where works involve minors.
- Verify institutional permissions where the submission is made on behalf of an institution.
- Record the rights status (Rights clear, Rights conditionally clear, Rights unclear, Rights blocked).
- Escalate rights conflicts to the Legal / Compliance Unit.

### 6.3. Authority Boundaries

- The Rights Administrator verifies rights status. They do not make final legal determinations.
- The Rights Administrator may not bypass the rights check for any work.
- Complex rights matters must be escalated to the Legal / Compliance Unit.

---

## 7. Cultural Review Unit

### 7.1. Role Definition

The Cultural Review Unit conducts deeper review for culturally sensitive, folklore-linked, community-linked, sacred, and restricted works.

### 7.2. Responsibilities

- Conduct the Cultural and Restricted Material Review (Stage 7) for all elevated works.
- Determine whether a work is modern, folklore-linked, or restricted.
- Assess whether underlying traditional expression is being reused without permission.
- Assess whether community-linked meaning or community-held cultural significance exists.
- Determine whether access should be limited, monetization should be blocked, or AI use must be denied.
- Assign a cultural status (No cultural restriction, Cultural condition, Cultural hold, Restricted, Escalate).
- Escalate to the Governance Board or to relevant external authorities where required.
- Manage community consultation where required.

### 7.3. Authority Boundaries

- The Cultural Review Unit conducts review and assigns cultural status. It does not hold final authority over matters escalated to the Governance Board.
- The Cultural Review Unit does not override Gambian law, ministerial authority, or community authority.
- The Cultural Review Unit must document all review decisions.

---

## 8. Legal / Compliance Unit

### 8.1. Role Definition

The Legal / Compliance Unit manages legal review, government authorization verification, and compliance matters within the AIWA system.

### 8.2. Responsibilities

- Conduct the Government Authorization Review (Stage 8) for all works claiming government approval.
- Verify all elements of a claimed government authorization as defined in AIWA-DOC-03 Stage 8.
- Assign an authorization status (Verified and scoped, Partially verified, Unverified, Invalid, Escalate externally).
- Handle government inquiries and requests.
- Manage contributor breach handling where government interaction is required (AIWA-DOC-03 Section 10.8).
- Escalate external legal and public authority matters to the appropriate outside authority.

### 8.3. Authority Boundaries

- The Legal / Compliance Unit verifies authorizations and manages legal matters. It does not hold legislative or judicial authority.
- The Legal / Compliance Unit may not represent that an authorization is valid until all verification elements are confirmed.
- Complex legal matters must be escalated to the Governance Board or outside counsel.

## 9. Decision Officer

### 9.1. Role Definition

The Decision Officer is the AIWA staff member responsible for issuing the formal operational decision at the Decision Gate (Stage 9).

### 9.2. Responsibilities

- Review all prior stage outputs before issuing a decision.
- Issue exactly one decision state from the six allowed states: Approved, Approved with Limits, Held, Restricted, Rejected, Escalated.
- Document the basis for the decision, the date, and any conditions or limits.
- Ensure that all limits are fully documented before issuing an Approved with Limits decision.
- Ensure that high-risk cases are not closed unilaterally.

### 9.3. Authority Boundaries

Decision Officer authority level is determined by case type:

| Case Type | Decision Authority |
|---|---|
| Standard cases | AIWA Operations staff with decision authority |
| Elevated cases | AIWA Senior Review officer |
| Restricted cases | AIWA Restricted-Material Review officer |
| Government-sensitive cases | AIWA Legal / Compliance Unit with required escalation |

- No Decision Officer may issue a decision on a high-risk case alone.
- No Decision Officer may issue a state outside the six allowed states.
- No Decision Officer may issue a verbal decision.

## 10. Monitoring and Compliance Team

### 10.1. Role Definition

The Monitoring and Compliance Team monitors all works after release or approval throughout their active life in the system.

### 10.2. Responsibilities

- Conduct Post-Release Monitoring (Stage 11) for all approved works.
- Track rights disputes, public objections, misuse, AI misuse, scope breaches, permission expiry, payout anomalies, community complaints, and government authorization status changes.
- Assign monitoring states (Stable, Watch, Review required, Immediate hold).
- Trigger formal review when monitoring state changes from Stable.
- Apply an Immediate Hold when required and escalate urgently.

### 10.3. Authority Boundaries

- The Monitoring and Compliance Team may apply an Immediate Hold on a monitoring basis.
- A formal Restricted or Rejected decision may only be issued through the Decision Gate.
- Monitoring matters escalate to the Decision Officer or Governance Board depending on severity.

---

## 11. Appeals Officer

### 11.1. Role Definition

The Appeals Officer manages formal appeals and correction requests submitted by contributors after a decision is issued.

### 11.2. Responsibilities

- Receive and review formal appeal requests.
- Confirm that the appeal is supported by reason, evidence, or an identified error.
- Conduct administrative reconsideration (first step of appeal).
- Refer defined high-stakes appeal matters to the Governance Board (second step of appeal).
- Maintain restriction on the work during the appeal unless formally lifted.
- Issue a new decision record upon completion of the appeal.

### 11.3. Authority Boundaries

- The Appeals Officer conducts administrative reconsideration only. Final appeal authority on high-stakes matters rests with the Governance Board.
- The Appeals Officer may not lift a restriction unilaterally on a high-stakes matter.
- All appeal decisions must be documented.

---

## 12. Quality and Governance Unit

### 12.1. Role Definition

The Quality and Governance Unit manages the audit, metrics, and process improvement function for the AIWA system.

### 12.2. Responsibilities

- Conduct Stage 13 Audit, Metrics, and Process Improvement on the fixed schedule defined by the Governance Board.
- Measure all metrics defined in AIWA-DOC-03 Stage 13.
- Identify bottlenecks, error patterns, and training gaps.
- Deliver written audit reports to the Governance Board.
- Recommend process improvements.

### 12.3. Authority Boundaries

- The Quality and Governance Unit reviews performance. It does not issue classification or operational decisions.
- Recommended process improvements require Governance Board approval before implementation.

---

## 13. PRIME Liaison

### 13.1. Role Definition

The PRIME Liaison manages the operating relationship between AIWA and PRIME.

### 13.2. Responsibilities

- Coordinate the transfer of approved works to PRIME for global exploitation.
- Confirm that PRIME operates only within the scope approved by AIWA for each work.
- Monitor PRIME's compliance with AIWA classification, restriction, and payout routing requirements.
- Escalate any PRIME boundary violation to the Governance Board.

### 13.3. Authority Boundaries

- The PRIME Liaison does not have authority to approve works for exploitation. Approval rests with the Decision Officer.
- The PRIME Liaison does not grant PRIME any rights beyond those assigned through the AIWA process.
- PRIME's scope of engagement does not expand beyond what is defined in the decision record for each work.

---

## 14. Submitting Party

### 14.1. Role Definition

The Submitting Party is any individual, group, institution, or authorized representative who submits a work to AIWA.

### 14.2. Responsibilities

- Submit works through authorized intake channels only.
- Provide accurate and complete information at intake.
- Affirm all five Confirmational Statements as defined in AIWA-DOC-01 Section 10.
- Bear primary responsibility for the accuracy of all declarations made at intake.
- Respond to requests for additional information within defined response windows.
- Comply with AIWA decisions regarding the work.

### 14.3. Authority Boundaries

- The Submitting Party has no authority over classification decisions.
- The Submitting Party may not instruct AIWA staff to bypass any stage.
- The Submitting Party's appeal rights are limited to the formal appeals process defined in AIWA-DOC-03 Stage 12.

---

## 15. PRIME

### 15.1. Role Definition

PRIME is the non-exclusive offshore rights and exploitation partner of AIWA. PRIME manages global rights exploitation, international licensing, platform distribution, cross-border monetization, and enforcement in applicable jurisdictions.

### 15.2. Responsibilities

- Execute global rights exploitation for works approved by AIWA for offshore distribution.
- Operate within the exact scope approved by AIWA for each work.
- Return revenue through the structured payout routing controlled by AIWA.
- Comply with AIWA classification, restriction, and AI eligibility decisions.
- Refrain from any action regarding works that have not been approved by AIWA.

### 15.3. Authority Boundaries

- PRIME does not control intake.
- PRIME does not control classification.
- PRIME does not control cultural designation.
- PRIME does not control restriction status.
- PRIME does not control local payout routing.
- PRIME may not expand the scope of use for any work beyond what AIWA has approved.
- PRIME may not bypass AIWA for any work that AIWA represents.
