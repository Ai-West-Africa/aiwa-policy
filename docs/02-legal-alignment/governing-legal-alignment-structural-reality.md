# Governing Legal Alignment & Structural Reality

**Document ID:** AIWA-DOC-02
**Version:** 1.0
**Scope:** Legal hierarchy, organizational structure, and structural relationships.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process or workflow.

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines the legal hierarchy governing AIWA operations.
1.2. This document defines the structural relationship between AIWA and PRIME.
1.3. This document defines the offshore and local structural arrangement.
1.4. This document defines ministry overlap and jurisdictional boundaries.

---

## 2. Legal Hierarchy

2.1. Gambian law governs first.
2.2. Gambian ministries and authorities apply within their designated jurisdictions.
2.3. AIWA governance applies within the scope of its mandate.
2.4. Contractual obligations apply between parties.
2.5. International frameworks apply in a supporting role only. They do not override Gambian law.
2.6. No contract may override Gambian law.
2.7. No international framework may override Gambian law in matters governed by Gambian statute.

---

## 3. Governing Law

3.1. All works registered in the AIWA system are subject to Gambian law.
3.2. All classifications made within the AIWA system are subject to Gambian law.
3.3. All licensing decisions within the AIWA system are subject to Gambian law.
3.4. Offshore operations of PRIME are subject to the law of PRIME's jurisdiction.
3.5. Offshore operations of PRIME do not override Gambian law for works governed locally.

---

## 4. AIWA — Structure and Role

4.1. AIWA is a Gambian-based publishing and media company.
4.2. AIWA is a copyright administrator.
4.3. AIWA is the cultural classification authority for works in this system.
4.4. AIWA is the stewardship and payout system for registered works.
4.5. AIWA is the controlled intake and governance layer for all works entering this system.
4.6. AIWA is the mandatory local steward. No work enters exploitation without AIWA classification and approval.
4.7. AIWA holds an ownership stake in the offshore structure.
4.8. AIWA's offshore ownership stake is not a majority stake.

---

## 5. PRIME — Structure and Role

5.1. PRIME is an offshore rights and exploitation partner.
5.2. PRIME is non-exclusive. PRIME does not hold exclusive global rights to any work.
5.3. PRIME is partially owned by the same founders as AIWA.
5.4. PRIME is not the local steward. PRIME does not perform local classification or intake.
5.5. PRIME operates under its offshore jurisdiction's law for its own operations.
5.6. PRIME's offshore operations remain subject to AIWA's contractual terms for works governed under this system.

---

## 6. Ownership Structure

6.1. Both founders — Max and Muhammed — are owners of the offshore structure.
6.2. Neither founder holds a majority stake in the offshore structure.
6.3. AIWA holds an ownership stake in the offshore structure.
6.4. Gambian ownership exists within the offshore structure.
6.5. Gambian ownership within the offshore structure is not a majority stake.
6.6. No single actor holds majority control over the system.
6.7. Foreign control of the system is not total.
6.8. Local control of the offshore structure is not total.

---

## 7. Ministry Jurisdiction

7.1. Multiple ministries may apply to works in this system.
7.2. No single ministry governs all work types.
7.3. Ministry overlap is expected and must be managed.
7.4. AIWA must identify the relevant ministry or ministries for each work type.
7.5. The following ministry jurisdictions may apply, depending on work type:

- Ministry responsible for culture and heritage — folklore, cultural works, and heritage content.
- Ministry responsible for information and media — broadcasting, radio, and television.
- Ministry responsible for education — educational materials and academic works.
- Ministry responsible for trade and industry — commercial exploitation and licensing.
- Ministry responsible for justice — legal enforcement and intellectual property rights.

7.6. When ministry jurisdiction is unclear, the work must be held until jurisdiction is confirmed.
7.7. AIWA must maintain a record of the ministry or ministries identified for each work.

---

## 8. Government Authorization

8.1. Government authorization is permission to use or exploit a work.
8.2. Government authorization is not ownership.
8.3. Government authorization does not remove the protection of a work.
8.4. Government authorization is scope-limited. It applies only to the scope defined in the authorization.
8.5. Government authorization must be verified before it is relied upon.
8.6. Government authorization does not override AIWA classification.
8.7. Government authorization does not authorize AI training use unless AI training use is explicitly stated in the authorization.
8.8. Government authorization does not constitute AIWA approval.

---

## 9. Classification Authority

9.1. AIWA holds classification authority for all works in this system.
9.2. Classification authority is not delegated to PRIME.
9.3. Classification authority is not delegated to government ministries.
9.4. Government authorization does not constitute classification.
9.5. A classified work may not be reclassified without a documented AIWA decision.

---

## 10. Offshore vs. Local Responsibility

10.1. Local intake, classification, and stewardship are AIWA's responsibility.
10.2. Offshore exploitation is managed through PRIME under AIWA's contractual terms.
10.3. AIWA must approve works before they are passed to PRIME for offshore exploitation.
10.4. PRIME must not exploit a work that AIWA has not approved.
10.5. PRIME must not reclassify a work.
10.6. PRIME must not override a restriction applied by AIWA.
