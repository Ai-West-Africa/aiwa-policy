# Cultural Resource & Classification — Definitions Layer

**Document ID:** AIWA-DOC-01
**Version:** 1.0
**Scope:** Definitions and classification boundaries only.
**Restrictions:** This document does not contain process, workflow, or procedures.

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document defines what constitutes a work under AIWA governance.
1.2. This document defines folklore.
1.3. This document defines the distinction between cultural works and modern creations.
1.4. This document establishes the boundaries between all classification types.
1.5. All classification decisions must reference this document.

---

## 2. Definition of a Work

2.1. A work is any original creation fixed in a tangible or digital medium.
2.2. A work must have an identifiable author or originating creator.
2.3. A work that lacks an identifiable author does not qualify as a modern work.
2.4. A work acquires protection at the point of creation and fixation.
2.5. A work does not require registration to receive protection under this framework.

---

## 3. Work Types

3.1. **Literary Work** — includes books, ebooks, written texts, and manuscripts.
3.2. **Musical Work** — includes musical compositions with or without lyrics.
3.3. **Sound Recording** — includes the recorded fixation of a musical or spoken performance.
3.4. **Audiovisual Work** — includes film, video, and television productions.
3.5. **Broadcast** — includes radio transmissions and television broadcasts.
3.6. **Educational Material** — includes instructional content in any fixed medium.
3.7. **Reference Work** — includes dictionaries, encyclopedias, and similar structured references.
3.8. **Dataset / Compilation** — includes structured collections of data or works selected and arranged by an identifiable party.
3.9. **AI Training Input** — includes any work used or intended for use in training an artificial intelligence model. AI training inputs are not clearly defined under current law. AI training inputs must be treated as controlled unless explicitly authorized.

---

## 4. Definition of Folklore

4.1. Folklore is a creation of collective origin.
4.2. Folklore has no identifiable author.
4.3. Folklore has been transmitted across generations within a community.
4.4. Folklore is not owned by any individual or private entity.
4.5. Folklore does not become privately owned through use, adaptation, or commercialization by a third party.
4.6. Government authorization of folklore use is permission only. It does not transfer ownership.
4.7. Gambian law governs the protection and use of folklore within this system.
4.8. The test for folklore classification requires both conditions to be met: collective origin AND absence of an identifiable author.

---

## 5. Cultural Work vs. Modern Creation

5.1. A modern work is not folklore.
5.2. Cultural influence does not make a work folklore.
5.3. A work created by an identifiable author is a modern work, regardless of cultural content.
5.4. A modern retelling of a folklore story is a modern work.
5.5. A modern work may draw from cultural themes, styles, or traditions. This does not reclassify it as folklore.
5.6. Cultural influence is a descriptor. Cultural influence is not a classification category.
5.7. The creator of a modern work retains full ownership of that work. Cultural content does not reduce or transfer that ownership.

---

## 6. Classification Categories

6.1. Every work entering the AIWA system must be assigned one primary classification.
6.2. The classification categories are:

- **Modern Work** — an original creation with an identifiable author.
- **Cultural Work (Modern)** — a modern work with demonstrable cultural content or context.
- **Folklore** — a creation of collective origin with no identifiable author.
- **Folklore-Linked Work** — a modern work that references, adapts, or builds upon folklore.
- **Dataset / Compilation** — a structured collection qualifying as a compilation under AIWA-STD-03.
- **AI Training Input** — any work designated or used for AI training purposes.
- **Unclassified** — a work that has not yet received a valid classification decision.

6.3. An Unclassified work must not be released, licensed, or exploited.
6.4. A work may only hold one primary classification at a time.
6.5. Reclassification must be recorded and authorized.
6.6. A work classified as AI Training Input may also carry a primary classification from another category. Both classifications must be recorded.

---

## 7. Boundaries and Restrictions

7.1. Cultural influence is not a classification. It is a descriptor.
7.2. A work's cultural content does not reduce the rights of its identifiable author.
7.3. Folklore may not be privately owned.
7.4. Folklore-linked works carry additional restrictions. These restrictions are defined in AIWA-STD-03 (Classification Matrix).
7.5. AI Training Inputs carry additional controls. These controls are defined in AIWA-STD-03 (Classification Matrix).
7.6. No work may bypass classification.
7.7. No classification may be reversed without a documented record.
7.8. When classification is uncertain, the work must be held as Unclassified until a valid decision is made.

---

## 8. Ownership Boundaries

8.1. AIWA is the mandatory local steward for all works in this system.
8.2. Modern works retain their creator's ownership.
8.3. AIWA stewardship does not replace creator ownership.
8.4. Folklore is not owned by AIWA, PRIME, or any individual party.
8.5. PRIME is a non-exclusive offshore partner. PRIME does not hold local stewardship.
8.6. No single actor holds majority control over the governance of this system.
8.7. Government authorization does not transfer ownership of any work.
