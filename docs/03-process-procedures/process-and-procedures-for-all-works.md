# Process & Procedures for All Works

**Document ID:** AIWA-DOC-03
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Scope:** System flow, stages, roles, escalation, government approval handling, system processing rights, and contributor breach handling.
**Restrictions:** This document is procedural and stage-based.
**Cross-Reference:** AIWA-DOC-01 (Classification), AIWA-DOC-02 (Legal Alignment), AIWA-GOV-01 (Roles), AIWA-GOV-02 (Decision Framework)

---

## 1. Purpose

1.1. This document sets the operating procedure for how AI West Africa (AIWA) handles all works from first contact to final status.

1.2. This document defines:

- what we do;
- why we do it;
- who does it;
- when it happens;
- where it happens;
- how it is documented;
- how decisions are checked; and
- how exceptions, disputes, government-authorized restricted works, and contributor breaches are handled.

1.3. This document is the procedural backbone of the AIWA system. No work may be approved, exploited, distributed, published, archived, placed in AI systems, or monetized without passing through the required stages defined in this document.

---

## 2. Core Operating Rule

2.1. Every work must move through a controlled sequence.

2.2. No work enters exploitation, AI use, publication, distribution, public presentation, or archive release without passing through the required stages.

2.3. No verbal decision counts.

2.4. No undocumented decision counts.

2.5. No one-person shortcut counts for any stage above standard administration level.

---

## 3. Operating Design Principles

3.1. This process follows six control principles:

1. **Intake before action** — No work is acted upon before it is formally received.
2. **Classification before exploitation** — No work is exploited, monetized, or distributed before classification is assigned.
3. **Documentation before approval** — No approval is valid without a documented record.
4. **Review before exception** — No exception to restriction is granted without a formal review.
5. **Restriction on doubt** — When in doubt, the work is restricted.
6. **Monitoring after release** — Approved use does not end oversight.

---

## 4. Scope

4.1. This procedure applies to all works submitted to AIWA, including:

- books and ebooks;
- educational materials;
- songs and musical compositions;
- sound recordings;
- films and audiovisual works;
- radio and television programmes;
- manuscripts;
- archives;
- dictionaries;
- encyclopedias;
- datasets;
- language resources;
- works submitted for educational language processing or archival use;
- culturally linked works;
- folklore-linked works; and
- government-authorized restricted works.

---

## 5. Roles

5.1. The following roles are responsible for executing this procedure. Full role definitions and authority boundaries are contained in AIWA-GOV-01.

| Role | Function |
|---|---|
| Intake Staff | Receive submissions, create intake records |
| Classification Officer | Assign and confirm classification categories |
| Rights Administrator | Verify submission authority and rights status |
| Cultural Review Unit | Conduct cultural, folklore, and community-linked review |
| Legal / Compliance Unit | Verify government authorizations, manage legal matters |
| Decision Officer | Issue formal decisions at the decision gate |
| Monitoring and Compliance Team | Monitor approved works post-release |
| Appeals Officer | Manage appeals and corrections |
| Quality and Governance Unit | Conduct audit, metrics, and process improvement |

5.2. Every stage must name the responsible role. No stage may proceed without an identified responsible party.

---

## 6. Allowed Decision States

6.1. The only allowed decision states throughout the AIWA system are:

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Approved** | Cleared for the stated scope of use |
| **Approved with Limits** | Cleared with defined restrictions on use |
| **Held** | Paused pending further information or review |
| **Restricted** | Blocked from the stated use |
| **Rejected** | Does not meet entry requirements |
| **Escalated** | Referred to the Governance Board or external authority |

6.2. No additional decision states are permitted.

6.3. Every decision must be recorded with exactly one decision state.

6.4. A decision state may only be changed through a new recorded decision.

6.5. No undocumented decisions are permitted.

6.6. No verbal approvals are permitted.

---

## 7. Process Architecture — 13 Controlled Stages

This procedure is divided into 13 controlled stages. Each stage produces a defined output. No stage may be skipped. No stages may be merged.

---

## Stage 1 — Intake

### 7.1.1. What

We receive the work and create the first controlled record.

### 7.1.2. Why

No work can be governed if it enters the system informally. Intake creates the first accountability anchor.

### 7.1.3. Who

AIWA Intake Staff.

### 7.1.4. When

Immediately upon submission by the contributor.

### 7.1.5. Where

Inside the controlled AIWA intake system.

### 7.1.6. How

The intake record must capture:

- contributor identity;
- title or provisional title;
- work type (as defined in AIWA-DOC-01 Section 3);
- date received;
- submission channel;
- claimed ownership status;
- collaborator status;
- cultural sensitivity declaration;
- folklore or restricted material declaration;
- government authorization claim, if any;
- intended use; and
- AI use request, if any.

All five Confirmational Statements (AIWA-DOC-01 Section 10) must be affirmed by the contributor before the intake record is created. Failure to affirm all five statements prevents progress past intake.

### 7.1.7. Output

**Intake ID created.** No approval issued at this stage.

---

## Stage 2 — Lexical Extraction

### 7.2.1. What

All submitted works are processed for lexical extraction.

### 7.2.2. Why

The system operates on words and contextual meaning structures as its core data layer. Lexical extraction provides the system with the internal data required for educational indexing, language resource development, and institutional language support. This extraction is independent of classification and independent of AI use permissions.

### 7.2.3. Who

System process under AIWA control.

### 7.2.4. When

Immediately after intake, before any classification decision.

### 7.2.5. Where

Within the AIWA processing system.

### 7.2.6. How

1. The system parses the submitted work.
2. The system extracts words, phrases, and contextual meaning structures.
3. The system stores the extracted data in structured form.
4. The system links the extracted data to the original work record.

Lexical extraction:

- occurs regardless of classification;
- applies to all work types, including restricted and folklore-linked works;
- does not reproduce or redistribute the original work;
- does not transfer ownership of the original work; and
- is not treated as optional AI use.

### 7.2.7. Output

**A lexical dataset linked to the work record.** The original work remains unchanged.

---

## Stage 3 — Completeness Check

### 7.3.1. What

We check whether the file, form, declarations, and attachments are complete enough to proceed.

### 7.3.2. Why

Incomplete works create false decisions later. Completeness must be confirmed before review resources are committed.

### 7.3.3. Who

AIWA Intake Review Staff.

### 7.3.4. When

Immediately after intake and lexical extraction.

### 7.3.5. Where

Inside the AIWA intake review system.

### 7.3.6. How

Use the following checklist:

- file is present;
- file is readable, playable, or usable;
- basic metadata is present;
- contributor declaration is present;
- rights statement is present;
- category clues are present; and
- required attachments are present.

### 7.3.7. Output

One of three statuses:

- **Complete** — proceed to Stage 4;
- **Incomplete** — returned to contributor for correction; or
- **Incomplete but holdable** — held with a defined response window for the contributor.

---

## Stage 4 — Initial Risk Screen

### 7.4.1. What

We identify whether the work is ordinary, culturally sensitive, restricted, or potentially escalated.

### 7.4.2. Why

Most harm comes from treating high-risk works like normal works. Risk identification at this stage ensures appropriate routing before classification is assigned.

### 7.4.3. Who

AIWA Classification Screen Staff.

### 7.4.4. When

After completeness check and before any classification decision.

### 7.4.5. Where

Inside the AIWA classification screening system.

### 7.4.6. How

Apply the following trigger questions:

- Is there an identifiable modern creator?
- Does the work appear to include traditional expressions?
- Does it contain sacred, ceremonial, communal, or ethnographic content?
- Does it involve minors or educational institutions?
- Is government approval being claimed?
- Is AI use being requested?
- Does the work create regulatory overlap across multiple ministries?

### 7.4.7. Output

One risk level:

- **Standard** — proceed to Stage 5;
- **Elevated** — proceed to Stage 5 with elevated flag;
- **Restricted-risk** — proceed to Stage 5 with restricted flag; or
- **Government-approval-risk** — proceed to Stage 5 with government-approval flag.

---

## Stage 5 — Initial Classification

### 7.5.1. What

We assign the work its first formal classification category.

### 7.5.2. Why

Treatment must follow class, not personal judgment. Classification anchors all subsequent handling decisions.

### 7.5.3. Who

AIWA Classification Officer.

### 7.5.4. When

After the initial risk screen.

### 7.5.5. Where

Inside the AIWA classification system.

### 7.5.6. How

The classification must use the categories defined in AIWA-DOC-01 Section 6:

- Class 1 — Original Individual Work;
- Class 2 — Culturally Derived Work;
- Class 3 — Community-Linked Work;
- Class 4 — Restricted Work; or
- Class 4A — Government-Authorized Restricted Work.

Low-confidence classification may not proceed directly to exploitation. It must proceed to further review.

### 7.5.7. Output

One initial class assigned, plus one confidence level:

- **High confidence** — proceed to Stage 6;
- **Medium confidence** — proceed to Stage 6 with review flag; or
- **Low confidence** — proceed to Stage 6 with mandatory secondary review flag.

---

## Stage 6 — Rights and Authority Check

### 7.6.1. What

We verify whether the person submitting has authority to place the work in the system.

### 7.6.2. Why

Authorship and submission authority are not always the same thing. Rights must be verified before any approval for use.

### 7.6.3. Who

AIWA Rights Administration Staff.

### 7.6.4. When

Before any approval for publication, distribution, archive release, AI use, or monetization.

### 7.6.5. Where

Inside the AIWA rights administration system.

### 7.6.6. How

Check all of the following:

- contributor authority to submit;
- co-author status and any co-author consents required;
- school or guardian permissions if the work involves minors;
- institutional permissions if the submission is on behalf of an institution;
- government approval documents if a government authorization is claimed;
- restrictions on use declared at intake; and
- conflicts with folklore or public-trust material identified in prior stages.

### 7.6.7. Output

One rights status:

- **Rights clear** — proceed to Stage 7;
- **Rights conditionally clear** — proceed to Stage 7 with conditions recorded;
- **Rights unclear** — hold pending clarification; or
- **Rights blocked** — restrict and notify contributor.

---

## Stage 7 — Cultural and Restricted Material Review

### 7.7.1. What

We conduct deeper review for culturally sensitive, folklore-linked, community-linked, sacred, or restricted works.

### 7.7.2. Why

Gambian rules on cultural materials may be broad in authority but thin in operating procedure. AIWA must have an internal procedure that is disciplined, reviewable, and respectful of cultural context.

### 7.7.3. Who

AIWA Cultural Review Unit.

### 7.7.4. When

Whenever a work is elevated beyond standard handling, as flagged in Stage 4 or Stage 5.

### 7.7.5. Where

Inside the AIWA cultural review system.

### 7.7.6. How

The review must examine:

- whether the work is modern, folklore-linked, or restricted;
- whether underlying traditional expression is being reused without permission;
- whether community-linked meaning exists;
- whether access should be limited;
- whether monetization should be blocked;
- whether AI use must be denied; and
- whether escalation to a ministry, community authority, or governance board is required.

### 7.7.7. Output

One cultural status:

- **No cultural restriction** — proceed to Stage 8;
- **Cultural condition** — proceed to Stage 8 with conditions recorded;
- **Cultural hold** — hold pending further information;
- **Restricted** — apply restriction and record; or
- **Escalate** — escalate to the appropriate authority.

---

## Stage 8 — Government Authorization Review

### 7.8.1. What

We verify any claimed government approval for restricted materials.

### 7.8.2. Why

Approval cannot be assumed, generalized, or treated as ownership transfer. A claimed approval must be verified before any restricted material proceeds.

### 7.8.3. Who

AIWA Legal / Compliance Unit, with escalation support where required.

### 7.8.4. When

Only when a work is a restricted material claim that relies on a claimed public authorization.

### 7.8.5. Where

Inside the AIWA legal and compliance review system.

### 7.8.6. How

Verify all of the following elements of the claimed authorization:

- issuing authority (name and title);
- date issued;
- scope of authorization;
- exact material covered;
- exact uses allowed;
- duration;
- any restrictions within the authorization;
- whether monetization is permitted;
- whether AI use is permitted; and
- revocation conditions.

If anything is unclear, incomplete, or unverified, the work stays restricted.

### 7.8.7. Output

One authorization status:

- **Verified and scoped** — proceed to Stage 9 with exact scope recorded;
- **Partially verified** — hold pending full verification;
- **Unverified** — remain restricted;
- **Invalid** — remain restricted and record the basis; or
- **Escalate externally** — refer to the relevant authority.

---

## Stage 9 — Decision Gate

### 7.9.1. What

We issue the first operational decision on the work.

### 7.9.2. Why

Every work must end review with a decision, not drift. The decision gate creates the formal accountability record.

### 7.9.3. Who

Assigned Decision Officer based on the level of the case:

- standard cases: AIWA Operations;
- elevated cases: AIWA Senior Review;
- restricted cases: AIWA Restricted-Material Review; or
- government-sensitive cases: AIWA Legal / Compliance Unit with required escalation path.

### 7.9.4. When

After all required prior stages are complete and outputs are recorded.

### 7.9.5. Where

Inside the AIWA decision management system.

### 7.9.6. How

Only the following outcomes are allowed:

- **Approved**;
- **Approved with Limits** (all limits must be documented);
- **Held** (pending more evidence — a defined response window must be set);
- **Restricted** (reason must be recorded);
- **Rejected** (reason must be recorded); or
- **Escalated** (destination and reason must be recorded).

No informal outcome is allowed. No verbal decision counts.

High-risk cases must not be closed by a single person.

### 7.9.7. Output

A formal decision record containing the decision state, the decision officer, the date, the basis, and any conditions or limits.

---

## Stage 10 — Implementation

### 7.10.1. What

We carry out the approved action.

### 7.10.2. Why

Approval without controlled implementation is meaningless. Implementation must follow the exact terms of the decision.

### 7.10.3. Who

The relevant operating team depending on the approved use:

- publishing team;
- archive team;
- education team;
- licensing team;
- AI and data team;
- communications team; or
- finance team.

### 7.10.4. When

Only after Decision Gate approval (Stage 9).

### 7.10.5. Where

Within the AIWA operating systems relevant to the approved use.

### 7.10.6. How

Implementation must follow the exact approval terms, including:

- where the work may be used;
- by whom the work may be used;
- for what purpose;
- under what limits;
- with what metadata attached;
- with what revenue routing; and
- with what AI permissions or prohibitions stated.

### 7.10.7. Output

The work is placed in the approved channel with full metadata, limits, and routing recorded.

---

## Stage 11 — Post-Release Monitoring

### 7.11.1. What

We monitor what happens after release or approval.

### 7.11.2. Why

Risk does not end at approval. Works may be misused, permissions may expire, and new disputes may arise. Monitoring is a continuing obligation.

### 7.11.3. Who

AIWA Monitoring and Compliance Team.

### 7.11.4. When

After approval and throughout the life of the work in the system.

### 7.11.5. Where

Across all channels where the work is active.

### 7.11.6. How

Track all of the following:

- rights disputes;
- public or community objections;
- misuse;
- AI misuse;
- breach of approved scope;
- expiry of permissions;
- payout anomalies;
- community complaints; and
- changes in government authorization status.

### 7.11.7. Output

One monitoring state:

- **Stable** — no action required;
- **Watch** — elevated monitoring assigned;
- **Review required** — formal review triggered; or
- **Immediate hold** — work held pending emergency review.

---

## Stage 12 — Appeals, Corrections, and Reopening

### 7.12.1. What

We allow formal review of disputed decisions.

### 7.12.2. Why

Error correction must exist, but under control. An appeal does not automatically change a decision.

### 7.12.3. Who

AIWA Appeals Officer, then higher review if required.

### 7.12.4. When

After a formal request is submitted, supported by reason, evidence, or an identified error.

### 7.12.5. Where

Inside the AIWA appeals management system.

### 7.12.6. How

A case may be reopened for:

- new evidence;
- identified classification error;
- rights correction;
- newly verified government authorization; or
- ministry or lawful authority intervention.

Restriction stays in place during the appeal unless formally lifted by the Appeals Officer or higher authority.

A two-step appeal process applies:

1. Administrative reconsideration by the Appeals Officer; then
2. Governance Board appeal for defined high-stakes matters.

### 7.12.7. Output

One of the allowed decision states applied to the reopened case, with a new decision record.

---

## Stage 13 — Audit, Metrics, and Process Improvement

### 7.13.1. What

We review how the system is performing.

### 7.13.2. Why

The procedure must be measurable, not just principled. The system must be repeatable, improvable, and reviewable.

### 7.13.3. Who

AIWA Quality and Governance Unit.

### 7.13.4. When

On a fixed schedule defined by the Governance Board.

### 7.13.5. Where

Across all stages of the AIWA process system.

### 7.13.6. How

Every review period must measure:

- number of works received;
- number of incomplete submissions;
- elevated-risk rate;
- restricted rate;
- number of government-authorization cases;
- appeal rate;
- reversal rate;
- rights dispute rate;
- turnaround time by stage;
- bottleneck stage identification;
- error patterns; and
- training gap identification.

### 7.13.7. Output

A written audit report delivered to the Governance Board, identifying performance metrics, identified risks, and required process improvements.

## 8. Escalation Map

8.1. The following escalation path applies to all matters arising within the AIWA system:

| Matter Type | Escalation Destination |
|---|---|
| Standard administrative issue | AIWA Operations |
| Rights issue | AIWA Rights Administration |
| Folklore, sacred, or community-linked issue | AIWA Cultural Review Unit |
| Government authorization issue | AIWA Legal / Compliance Unit |

8.2. A matter may move through multiple escalation levels before resolution.

8.3. The escalation path does not skip levels except where the matter directly involves external legal authority.

## 9. System Processing Rights

9.1. **All submitted material is processed into words, phrases, and contextual meaning structures.** Agreement to this processing is established by the contributor's acceptance of submission conditions at intake. Processing is not tied to educational system use permission settings.

9.2. By submitting a work to AIWA and accepting the submission conditions at intake, the contributor grants AIWA a non-exclusive, royalty-free, revocable where applicable license to:

- parse, classify, index, store, and administer submitted works for the purposes defined within the AIWA system;
- extract linguistic elements and derive contextual meaning structures;
- retain extracted data within internal AIWA educational and archival systems; and
- use classified and indexed works within the scope approved through the AIWA decision process.

The contributor retains ownership of the original submitted work. This license does not constitute a transfer of ownership.

9.3. This right applies to:

- published works;
- unpublished works;
- restricted works; and
- removed works.

9.4. **Separation of Rights:**

| Layer | Owner | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Original Work | Contributor | Subject to publishing, distribution, and restriction rules |
| Extracted Language Data | AIWA (rights in structured representation and arrangement) | Not the original work; not a substitute for the original work; AIWA holds rights in the compiled structure, not in individual linguistic elements |

9.5. Submission to AIWA constitutes the contributor's agreement to system processing. If a contributor does not agree to system processing, the contributor must not submit to AIWA.

9.6. System processing does not:

- transfer ownership of the original work;
- grant AIWA ownership of the original work; or
- create a claim over the creator's authorship.

9.7. If a work is removed, restricted, or disputed, the system may:

- retain extracted linguistic data;
- retain the intake and review records; and
- retain the audit history.

9.8. If challenged, AIWA's position is:

- the contributor submitted voluntarily;
- the contributor agreed to processing at intake;
- system processing is distinct from publication or distribution; and
- system processing does not replace or reproduce the original work.

## 10. Contributor Breach Handling

### 10.1. Definition of Breach

10.1.1. A breach occurs when a contributor submits material that:

- AIWA has explicitly prohibited;
- is materially misrepresented as to its nature, origin, or rights status;
- is submitted without the required rights or authority; or
- violates stated submission rules.

### 10.2. Primary Responsibility

10.2.1. The contributor is solely responsible for:

- authorship claims;
- rights claims;
- origin claims; and
- compliance with submission rules.

10.2.2. AIWA relies on contributor declarations made at intake. Those declarations transfer primary responsibility to the contributor.

### 10.3. AIWA Position

10.3.1. AIWA:

- does not verify all claims prior to processing;
- does not act as a cultural authority on all submissions; and
- does not assume the truth of declarations beyond the intake record.

10.3.2. AIWA acts as:

- publisher;
- administrator; and
- processor.

### 10.4. Detection Triggers

10.4.1. A breach may be identified through:

- internal review at any stage;
- external complaint from a third party;
- government inquiry; or
- system flag.

### 10.5. Immediate Actions

10.5.1. Upon breach detection, AIWA must:

- assign breach status to the work record;
- restrict or hold the work immediately;
- prevent further distribution if required; and
- preserve all records.

### 10.6. Contributor Notice

10.6.1. AIWA must:

- notify the contributor of the breach status;
- request clarification or response; and
- provide a defined response window.

### 10.7. Resolution Outcomes

10.7.1. Only the following outcomes are allowed upon resolution of a breach:

- **Reinstate** — work is restored to its prior status;
- **Reinstate with limits** — work is restored with defined conditions;
- **Maintain restriction** — work remains restricted;
- **Remove** — work is removed from the system;
- **Escalate** — matter is referred to higher authority; or
- **Suspend contributor** — contributor's access is suspended pending further determination.

### 10.8. Government Interaction

10.8.1. If a government authority contacts AIWA regarding a contributor's work, AIWA:

- provides contributor identity, submission records, declarations, and system actions taken;
- may comply with lawful requests; and
- may restrict or remove content as required by lawful direction.

10.8.2. AIWA does not:

- assume contributor guilt before determination; or
- assume authority beyond what the law grants.

10.8.3. Notice to Contributor. Where a government authority contacts AIWA regarding a contributor's work, AIWA will notify the relevant contributor of the inquiry as promptly as practicable, unless: (a) the applicable government authority has directed AIWA not to disclose the inquiry; (b) applicable law prohibits such disclosure; or (c) AIWA's legal counsel advises that disclosure would create legal risk for AIWA or would interfere with a lawful investigation. Where notice cannot be given immediately, AIWA will provide notice at the earliest point that disclosure is permissible.

### 10.9. Record Preservation

10.9.1. AIWA must retain throughout the breach handling process:

- the original submission;
- extracted linguistic data;
- contributor declarations;
- communication logs; and
- decision records.

### 10.10. AIWA's Position on Contributor Breach

10.10.1 AIWA does not assume primary responsibility for a contributor's breach of submission rules, misrepresentation of rights, or false declaration of ownership or origin. Primary responsibility for the accuracy of submissions rests with the contributor.

10.10.2 AIWA is responsible for:

(a) documented response to identified breaches;

(b) controlled handling in accordance with the procedures defined in this document; and

(c) lawful compliance with applicable Gambian law and any applicable governmental direction.

10.10.3 Nothing in this Section limits AIWA's liability to the extent that such liability cannot be excluded under applicable Gambian law or other applicable law. AIWA's liability for its own operational failures, documentation failures, or procedural violations is governed by AIWA's applicable liability framework.

## 11. Control Rules

11.1. The following procedural rules apply at all times and to all works in the AIWA system:

11.1.1. No undocumented approval.

11.1.2. No verbal override.

11.1.3. No release before classification.

11.1.4. No AI use before rights and cultural clearance.

11.1.5. A restricted work does not become ordinary because someone states it is approved. Approval must be formally verified and recorded.

11.1.6. No appeal automatically lifts a hold. A hold remains until a new recorded decision lifts it.

11.1.7. No expired authorization remains active. Expiry must be tracked and acted upon.

11.1.8. No one person may close a high-risk case alone.

11.1.9. No bypass of classification for any reason.

11.1.10. When in doubt, restrict.
